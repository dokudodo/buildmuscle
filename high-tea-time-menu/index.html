If you're looking for a way to unwind and enjoy a delightful afternoon with your friends or family, then high tea time is the perfect option. High tea time is a British tradition that dates back to the 19th century where it was known as afternoon tea. It has become a popular social event worldwide, and it's a perfect opportunity to indulge in delicious treats and quality tea. In this article, we'll explore the high tea time menu, including how to prepare it, step by step, tips, and pros and cons.

What is High Tea Time?

High tea time is a formal afternoon event that usually takes place between 3 pm to 5 pm. It's a time to relax, socialize, and enjoy a variety of sweet and savory treats, along with a hot cup of tea. The menu usually includes finger sandwiches, scones, cakes, and pastries. High tea time is perfect for intimate gatherings, bridal showers, birthdays, or any other special occasion.

How to Prepare High Tea Time Menu?

Preparing a high tea time menu might seem daunting, but it's simple and fun. Before you start, make sure you have all the necessary equipment such as teapots, cups, and saucers. Here is a step-by-step guide to help you prepare a high tea time menu:

Step 1: Choose Your Tea

The first step in preparing a high tea time menu is to choose your tea. Tea is the centerpiece of the event, and it's essential to choose a high-quality tea. Some popular tea flavors for high tea time include Earl Grey, English Breakfast, and Darjeeling. Make sure you have enough tea for your guests and keep some extra hot water handy in case anyone wants to dilute their tea.

Step 2: Prepare Finger Sandwiches

Finger sandwiches are a staple of high tea time. They're small, easy to eat, and come in a variety of flavors. Some popular sandwich fillings include cucumber and cream cheese, smoked salmon, and egg mayonnaise. Cut the sandwiches into small triangles or rectangles and serve them on a decorative plate.

Step 3: Bake Scones

Scones are another essential item on the high tea time menu. They're easy to bake, and you can make them in advance. Serve them with clotted cream and jam or lemon curd. You can also add some fruit or nuts to the scones for extra flavor.

Step 4: Prepare Cakes and Pastries

Cakes and pastries are the final touch to the high tea time menu. You can bake them at home or buy them from your local bakery. Some popular options include cupcakes, macarons, and mini fruit tarts. Serve them on a tiered cake stand for an elegant touch.

Tips for a Perfect High Tea Time

Here are some tips to help you make the most out of your high tea time:

  • Set the table with elegant teapots, cups, and saucers.
  • Use a tiered cake stand to display the food.
  • Label the food items for your guests.
  • Offer a variety of tea flavors.
  • Serve your tea in a teapot with a strainer to prevent loose tea leaves from getting into the cup.
  • Provide napkins and small plates for your guests.

Pros and Cons of High Tea Time

Like any other event, high tea time has its pros and cons:

Pros:

  • It's a great way to socialize with friends and family.
  • It's a perfect option for intimate gatherings.
  • You get to indulge in delicious treats and high-quality tea.
  • It's a fun and relaxing way to spend an afternoon.

Cons:

  • It can be expensive if you choose to buy high-quality tea and food items.
  • It requires some preparation and planning.
  • You might have to clean up afterward.

FAQs

What is the Difference between High Tea and Afternoon Tea?

High tea is a more substantial meal that includes hot dishes, such as meat pies, sausage rolls, and baked beans, in addition to the usual sandwiches, scones, and cakes. Afternoon tea, on the other hand, is a lighter meal that includes sandwiches, scones, and cakes, along with tea.

How Many Sandwiches Should I Make for High Tea Time?

Plan for three to four sandwiches per person, depending on their appetite.

Can I Use Herbal Tea for High Tea Time?

While herbal tea is an excellent option for tea lovers, it's not traditional for high tea time. Stick to classic tea flavors such as Earl Grey or English Breakfast.

Final Thoughts

High tea time is a delightful tradition that can be enjoyed with family and friends. It's a perfect opportunity to unwind and indulge in delicious treats and high-quality tea. With a little bit of planning and preparation, you can create a memorable high tea time experience that everyone will enjoy.
