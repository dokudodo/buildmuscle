Cholesterol is a type of fat that is necessary for the functioning of our body. However, when the level of cholesterol increases in our body, it can lead to several health problems, including heart disease and stroke. To reduce cholesterol levels, one can follow a healthy diet that is low in saturated and trans fats. In this article, we will discuss the Indian diet to reduce cholesterol naturally.

What is cholesterol?

Cholesterol is a waxy substance that is present in all the cells of our body. It is essential for the production of hormones, vitamin D, and bile acids that help in the digestion of fats. Cholesterol is produced by our liver and can also be obtained from the food we eat.

Types of cholesterol

There are two types of cholesterol:

  • Low-density lipoprotein (LDL): This is known as the "bad" cholesterol as it can build up in the walls of our arteries and form plaques that can increase the risk of heart disease and stroke.
  • High-density lipoprotein (HDL): This is known as the "good" cholesterol as it helps to remove the excess cholesterol from our arteries and transport it to the liver for excretion.

What causes high cholesterol?

Several factors can contribute to high cholesterol levels in our body, including:

  • Diet high in saturated and trans fats
  • Obesity
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Smoking
  • Genetics

Indian diet to reduce cholesterol

Following an Indian diet that is low in saturated and trans fats can help to reduce cholesterol levels naturally. Here are some foods that you can include in your diet:

  • Fruits and vegetables: Include a variety of fruits and vegetables in your diet as they are high in fiber, antioxidants, and vitamins.
  • Whole grains: Choose whole grains such as brown rice, whole wheat, and oats instead of refined grains.
  • Legumes: Include lentils, beans, and chickpeas in your diet as they are high in fiber and protein.
  • Nuts and seeds: Include almonds, walnuts, chia seeds, and flax seeds in your diet as they are high in healthy fats and fiber.
  • Fish: Include fatty fish such as salmon and mackerel in your diet as they are high in omega-3 fatty acids that can help to reduce inflammation and lower cholesterol levels.
  • Spices: Include spices such as turmeric, ginger, and cinnamon in your diet as they have anti-inflammatory properties and can help to lower cholesterol levels.

Foods to avoid

To reduce cholesterol levels, it is important to avoid foods that are high in saturated and trans fats. Here are some foods that you should limit or avoid:

  • Red meat
  • Processed meats such as sausages and bacon
  • Fried foods
  • Butter and ghee
  • Full-fat dairy products
  • Bakery products such as cakes and pastries
  • Fast food

Tips to reduce cholesterol

Here are some tips that can help to reduce cholesterol levels:

  • Reduce intake of saturated and trans fats
  • Include more fiber in your diet
  • Limit intake of sugar and refined grains
  • Drink plenty of water
  • Avoid smoking
  • Limit alcohol intake

Exercise to reduce cholesterol

Along with a healthy diet, regular exercise can also help to reduce cholesterol levels. Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise such as brisk walking, cycling, or swimming on most days of the week.

FAQ

Q. Can I eat eggs if I have high cholesterol?

A. Yes, you can eat eggs in moderation as they are a good source of protein. However, it is recommended to limit intake of egg yolks to 3-4 per week as they are high in cholesterol.

Q. Can I eat red meat if I have high cholesterol?

A. It is recommended to limit intake of red meat as it is high in saturated fat. Instead, choose lean meats such as chicken and fish.

Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • Following an Indian diet to reduce cholesterol levels can improve overall health.
  • It is a natural way to reduce cholesterol levels without the use of medication.
  • It can also help to reduce the risk of other health problems such as diabetes and obesity.

Cons:

  • It may be difficult to follow the diet if you are used to eating a lot of processed and fried foods.
  • It may take some time to see results.
  • If you have severe high cholesterol levels, you may need medication in addition to following a healthy diet.

Conclusion

Following an Indian diet that is low in saturated and trans fats can help to reduce cholesterol levels naturally. Include a variety of fruits and vegetables, whole grains, legumes, nuts, seeds, and fatty fish in your diet. Limit intake of red meat, fried foods, and processed foods. Exercise regularly and follow other tips to reduce cholesterol levels. If you have severe high cholesterol levels, consult your doctor for medication in addition to following a healthy diet.
