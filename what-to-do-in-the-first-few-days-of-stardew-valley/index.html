Welcome to Stardew Valley! If you're new to the game, you might be wondering what you should be doing in the first few days. This guide will give you some tips on how to get started and make the most out of your time in the Valley.

Day 1

On your first day, you'll start off in your farmhouse. The first thing you should do is check your TV for the weather report. This will tell you if it's going to rain or not, which will affect your plans for the day.

Next, you should head outside and start clearing out your farm. Use your scythe to clear out any weeds or grass, and use your axe to chop down any trees or stumps in your way. You can also gather some wood and forage for some items like berries or wild onions.

Once you've cleared out some space, you can start planting some crops. You'll start off with a few seeds, which you can plant in the tilled soil. Make sure to water them afterwards, and keep an eye on them over the next few days.

Day 2

On your second day, you should continue to clear out your farm and plant more crops. You'll also want to head into town to meet some of the locals. You can visit the general store to buy more seeds and supplies, or you can stop by the saloon to grab a drink and chat with the regulars.

While you're in town, you should also stop by the beach and do some fishing. You can catch fish to sell for extra money, or you can use them to cook meals to boost your energy and health.

Day 3

By your third day, you should start to see some of your crops sprouting up. Make sure to water them every day, and use your hoe to till more soil for planting. You can also start to build some basic structures, like a coop for chickens or a barn for cows.

You should also start to think about making some friends in the Valley. You can give gifts to the locals to increase their affection towards you, and you can also take on some quests from the bulletin board in the town square.

Day 4

On your fourth day, you'll want to continue to tend to your crops and animals. You can also start to explore some of the different areas in the Valley, like the mines or the forest. These areas have different resources and enemies, so make sure to be prepared with weapons and tools.

You can also start to upgrade your tools by visiting the blacksmith in town. This will cost you some money and resources, but it will make your tools more efficient and effective.

Day 5

By your fifth day, you should have a good routine established for tending to your farm and making friends in the Valley. You can continue to explore and upgrade your tools, or you can start to focus on other goals, like completing the community center or getting married.

Remember, there's no set goal or timeline in Stardew Valley. Take your time and enjoy the experience!

Tips

  • Focus on one or two crops at first, so you don't get overwhelmed.
  • Don't forget to water your crops every day!
  • Take breaks from farming to explore and make friends.
  • Upgrade your tools as soon as you can.
  • Use the community center to track your progress and get rewards.

FAQ

Q: How do I make money in Stardew Valley?

A: You can make money by selling crops, animals, and fish. You can also complete quests and sell items you find in the mines or the forest.

Q: Can I play Stardew Valley with friends?

A: Yes, you can play multiplayer with up to three other players.

Q: How do I get married in Stardew Valley?

A: You need to increase your affection with one of the eligible bachelors or bachelorettes, and then give them a bouquet to express your love. Once they accept, you can start dating and eventually get married.

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Relaxing and enjoyable gameplay
  • Endless possibilities and goals
  • Fun multiplayer mode

Cons

  • Can be slow-paced and repetitive
  • Some players may find it too simplistic
  • May not appeal to all gamers
