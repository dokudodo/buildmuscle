Las Vegas, also known as Sin City, has a reputation for being a party destination. However, there are plenty of things to do around Las Vegas during the day that don't involve gambling or drinking. Whether you're a nature lover or a history buff, there's something for everyone in and around Las Vegas. In this article, we'll explore some of the top activities and attractions to check out during the day.

How To Get Around Las Vegas

Before we dive into the top things to do around Las Vegas during the day, it's important to know how to get around the city. The easiest and most convenient way to get around Las Vegas is by car. There are plenty of rental car companies located at the airport, and parking is plentiful at most attractions. If you don't want to rent a car, there are also taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft available.

Top Things To Do Around Las Vegas During The Day

1. Visit Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area

If you love the great outdoors, a trip to Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area is a must-do. Located just 17 miles west of the Las Vegas Strip, Red Rock Canyon is a beautiful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. The park is home to stunning red rock formations, hiking trails, and wildlife.

2. Explore The Hoover Dam

The Hoover Dam is a marvel of engineering that's located just a short drive from Las Vegas. The dam was built during the Great Depression and is still one of the largest dams in the world. Visitors can take a tour of the dam and learn about its history and construction.

3. Check Out The Neon Museum

The Neon Museum is a unique museum that's dedicated to preserving the iconic neon signs of Las Vegas. The museum is located in downtown Las Vegas and features over 200 restored signs from the 1930s to the present day.

4. Visit The Mob Museum

The Mob Museum is a fascinating museum that's dedicated to the history of organized crime in America. The museum is located in downtown Las Vegas and features exhibits on famous gangsters like Al Capone and John Gotti.

5. Take A Helicopter Tour Of The Grand Canyon

If you're looking for a once-in-a-lifetime experience, a helicopter tour of the Grand Canyon is a must-do. There are several companies that offer tours from Las Vegas, and most tours include a landing in the canyon for a picnic lunch.

Tips For Visiting Las Vegas During The Day

Here are some tips to make the most of your visit to Las Vegas during the day:

  • Wear comfortable shoes, especially if you're planning on doing a lot of walking or hiking.
  • Bring sunscreen and plenty of water, as the desert sun can be intense.
  • Plan ahead and make reservations for popular attractions and tours.
  • Consider visiting during the offseason to avoid crowds and save money on accommodations.

Pros And Cons Of Visiting Las Vegas During The Day

Pros

  • Less crowded than at night
  • More opportunities to explore the city's history and natural beauty
  • Less expensive than visiting the city's nightclubs and shows

Cons

  • The desert heat can be intense during the day, especially during the summer months
  • Some attractions may have limited hours or be closed during the day
  • Less of a party atmosphere than at night

FAQ

What is there to do in Las Vegas during the day besides gambling?

There are plenty of things to do in Las Vegas during the day besides gambling, including visiting Red Rock Canyon, exploring the Neon Museum, and taking a helicopter tour of the Grand Canyon.

Is it safe to visit Las Vegas during the day?

Yes, Las Vegas is generally a safe city to visit during the day. However, as with any city, it's important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to stay safe.

What should I wear when visiting Las Vegas during the day?

It's important to wear comfortable clothing and shoes when visiting Las Vegas during the day. The desert sun can be intense, so be sure to wear sunscreen and bring plenty of water.

Conclusion

Las Vegas may be known for its nightlife, but there are plenty of things to do around the city during the day. From exploring the natural beauty of Red Rock Canyon to learning about the city's history at the Mob Museum, there's something for everyone in Las Vegas. So next time you're planning a trip to Sin City, be sure to check out some of these top attractions and activities.
