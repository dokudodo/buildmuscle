Description

The back of your arms, also known as the triceps, is an important muscle group that helps with arm extension and pushing motions. While many exercises work the triceps, some are more effective at targeting the back of your arms specifically.

How to

The best exercise to work the back of your arms is the tricep extension. This exercise can be done with dumbbells, a cable machine, or resistance bands. To perform the exercise:

With dumbbells:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a dumbbell in each hand.
  2. Extend your arms straight above your head, keeping your elbows close to your ears.
  3. Bend your elbows to slowly lower the dumbbells behind your head.
  4. Pause briefly, then straighten your arms to return to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for 10-12 reps and 2-3 sets.

With a cable machine:

  1. Attach a straight bar to a high pulley on a cable machine.
  2. Stand facing the machine with your feet shoulder-width apart.
  3. Grasp the bar with an overhand grip and extend your arms straight above your head.
  4. Bend your elbows to slowly lower the bar behind your head.
  5. Pause briefly, then straighten your arms to return to the starting position.
  6. Repeat for 10-12 reps and 2-3 sets.

With resistance bands:

  1. Attach a resistance band to a sturdy object above your head.
  2. Stand facing away from the object with your feet shoulder-width apart.
  3. Grasp the band with an overhand grip and extend your arms straight above your head.
  4. Bend your elbows to slowly lower your hands behind your head.
  5. Pause briefly, then straighten your arms to return to the starting position.
  6. Repeat for 10-12 reps and 2-3 sets.

Step by step

The tricep extension can be broken down into the following steps:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a weight or resistance band in your hands.
  2. Extend your arms straight above your head, keeping your elbows close to your ears.
  3. Bend your elbows to slowly lower the weight or band behind your head.
  4. Pause briefly, then straighten your arms to return to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for 10-12 reps and 2-3 sets.

Tips

To maximize the effectiveness of tricep extensions, follow these tips:

  • Use a weight or resistance band that is challenging but allows you to maintain proper form.
  • Keep your elbows close to your head throughout the exercise.
  • Engage your core muscles to maintain stability.
  • Exhale as you extend your arms and inhale as you lower the weight or band.

Solution

If you're looking to target the back of your arms specifically, the tricep extension is the best exercise to do so. Incorporate it into your arm workout routine for best results.

FAQ

What other exercises work the back of your arms?

Other exercises that work the back of your arms include tricep dips, tricep pushdowns, and close-grip bench presses.

How often should I do tricep extensions?

You can do tricep extensions 2-3 times per week as part of your arm workout routine.

Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • Effective at targeting the back of your arms
  • Can be done with dumbbells, a cable machine, or resistance bands
  • Can be easily incorporated into your arm workout routine

Cons:

  • May require equipment if not using bodyweight
  • Can cause strain on the elbows if proper form is not maintained
  • May not be suitable for those with pre-existing elbow or shoulder injuries
