Veggie Fried Rice

This recipe is a great way to use leftover rice and veggies. It's quick, easy, and healthy.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (frozen or fresh)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 egg (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat the oil in a pan over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the garlic and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  3. Add the vegetables and cook until tender, about 5 minutes.
  4. Push the vegetables to one side of the pan and crack the egg into the other side.
  5. Scramble the egg and mix it with the vegetables.
  6. Add the rice and soy sauce and stir until everything is well combined.
  7. Cook for another 5 minutes or until the rice is heated through.

Enjoy!

Sweet Potato and Black Bean Tacos

These tacos are a great way to get your protein and fiber in. Plus, they're super tasty!

Ingredients

  • 1 sweet potato, peeled and diced
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Tortillas
  • Optional toppings: avocado, salsa, cilantro, lime

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. Toss the sweet potato with a drizzle of oil and some salt and pepper.
  3. Roast for 20-25 minutes or until tender.
  4. Meanwhile, sauté the onion and garlic in a pan until softened.
  5. Add the black beans, chili powder, cumin, and some salt and pepper.
  6. Heat until the beans are warmed through.
  7. Warm the tortillas in the oven or microwave.
  8. Assemble the tacos with the sweet potato and black bean mixture.
  9. Add any desired toppings.

Enjoy!

Mushroom and Spinach Quesadillas

Quesadillas are a great go-to meal when you're short on time. This recipe is packed with veggies and flavor.

Ingredients

  • 4 tortillas
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 2 cups spinach
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Sour cream and salsa for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. Sauté the mushrooms, onion, and garlic in a pan until the mushrooms are cooked and the onion is softened.
  2. Add the spinach and cook until wilted.
  3. Remove the veggies from the pan and wipe the pan clean.
  4. Place a tortilla in the pan over medium-high heat.
  5. Sprinkle half of the tortilla with cheese.
  6. Add some of the veggie mixture on top of the cheese.
  7. Fold the tortilla in half and cook until the cheese is melted and the tortilla is crispy.
  8. Repeat with the remaining tortillas and filling.
  9. Serve with sour cream and salsa if desired.

Enjoy!

Pesto Pasta with Roasted Tomatoes

This pasta dish is a great way to use up any leftover veggies you have in the fridge. The roasted tomatoes add a nice pop of flavor.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz pasta
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup pesto
  • Optional toppings: Parmesan cheese, pine nuts, basil

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. Toss the cherry tomatoes with 1 tbsp of olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Roast for 15-20 minutes or until the tomatoes are soft and blistered.
  4. Cook the pasta according to package directions.
  5. Drain the pasta and toss with the remaining 1 tbsp of olive oil and pesto.
  6. Add the roasted tomatoes and any desired toppings.

Enjoy!

Chickpea and Vegetable Curry

This curry is packed with protein and veggies. It's also great for meal prepping and can be made in big batches.

Ingredients

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice for serving

Instructions

  1. Sauté the onion and garlic in a pan until softened.
  2. Add the bell pepper and zucchini and sauté until tender.
  3. Stir in the chickpeas, vegetable broth, diced tomatoes, curry powder, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  4. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Serve over cooked rice.

Enjoy!

Tips for Making Quick and Easy Vegetarian Dinners

  • Plan ahead and make a grocery list before you go shopping.
  • Buy frozen veggies and canned beans to save time.
  • Invest in some basic spices like chili powder, cumin, and curry powder.
  • Meal prep on the weekends to save time during the week.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different ingredients and flavors.

FAQ

What are some good vegetarian sources of protein?

Some good vegetarian sources of protein include beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, nuts, and seeds.

How can I make sure I'm getting enough nutrients on a vegetarian diet?

Make sure to eat a variety of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and protein sources. Consider taking a multivitamin or talking to a registered dietitian for personalized advice.

  • Can be more environmentally sustainable
