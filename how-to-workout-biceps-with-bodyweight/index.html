How To Workout Biceps With Bodyweight A Step By Step Guide

Written by April Dec 31, 2022 · 4 min read

.

Are you looking to improve your arm strength but don't have access to weights? Not to worry, there are plenty of bodyweight exercises that can help you build biceps and increase your overall arm strength. In this article, we will discuss how to workout biceps with bodyweight exercises, providing a solution for those without access to gym equipment.

How to Workout Biceps with Bodyweight

Bodyweight exercises are a great way to build biceps without having to use weights. Here are some of the best bodyweight exercises you can do to build bicep strength:
  • Push-ups
  • Chin-ups
  • Dips
  • Inverted Rows
  • Handstand Push-ups

Step-by-Step Guide

Let's take a closer look at each exercise and how to properly perform them for maximum bicep gains. Push-ups: Begin in a plank position with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Lower your body until your chest nearly touches the ground, and then push yourself back up to the starting position. This will work your chest, shoulders, and triceps, as well as your biceps. Chin-ups: Grab a pull-up bar with your palms facing towards you and your hands shoulder-width apart. Pull your body up until your chin is above the bar, then slowly lower yourself back down to the starting position. This exercise primarily targets your biceps and back muscles. Dips: Find parallel bars or two sturdy chairs and place your hands on them, shoulder-width apart. Lower your body until your elbows are at a 90-degree angle, and then push yourself back up to the starting position. This exercise targets your triceps and chest, as well as your biceps. Inverted Rows: Lie underneath a sturdy horizontal bar, such as a table or a Smith machine. Grab the bar with an overhand grip and lift your body up towards the bar, keeping your elbows tucked in. Lower yourself back down to the starting position. This exercise targets your biceps and back muscles. Handstand Push-ups: Begin in a handstand position against a wall, keeping your hands shoulder-width apart. Lower your body until your head nearly touches the ground, and then push yourself back up to the starting position. This exercise primarily targets your shoulders, but also works your biceps and triceps.

Tips for Effective Bicep Workouts

To get the most out of your bodyweight bicep workouts, follow these tips:
  • Use proper form to prevent injury
  • Incorporate a variety of exercises for maximum gains
  • Gradually increase the number of reps and sets for each exercise
  • Take rest days to allow your muscles time to recover
  • Eat a balanced diet to support muscle growth

Solution for Building Bicep Strength

Bodyweight exercises can be just as effective as weightlifting for building bicep strength. By incorporating a variety of exercises and following proper form, you can achieve the same results without having to use weights.

FAQs

Q: Can bodyweight exercises really build bicep strength?
A: Yes, bodyweight exercises can be just as effective as weightlifting for building bicep strength. Q: How often should I do bodyweight bicep workouts?
A: It's recommended to do bodyweight bicep workouts 2-3 times per week, with rest days in between.

Pros and Cons of Bodyweight Bicep Workouts

Pros:
  • No need for gym equipment
  • Can be done anywhere, anytime
  • Effective for building bicep strength
Cons:
  • May be difficult to progress without weights
  • May not be as effective as weightlifting for overall muscle growth
  • Requires proper form to prevent injury
In conclusion, bodyweight exercises can be a great solution for those looking to build bicep strength without having access to gym equipment. By incorporating a variety of exercises and following proper form, you can achieve the same results as weightlifting. Remember to gradually increase the number of reps and sets for each exercise, take rest days, and eat a balanced diet to support muscle growth.
