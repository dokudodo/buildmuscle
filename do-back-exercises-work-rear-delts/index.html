Description

Before we jump into the nitty-gritty of whether back exercises work rear delts, let's first define what we mean by "rear delts." The rear deltoid muscle is one of the three heads of the deltoid muscle. It's located at the back of your shoulder and is responsible for shoulder extension, external rotation, and transverse abduction.

Now that we know what the rear delts are, let's talk about back exercises. Back exercises are movements that target the muscles in your back, including the lats, traps, and rhomboids. Examples of back exercises include pull-ups, rows, and lat pulldowns.

How to

The best way to work your rear delts is to perform exercises that specifically target them. While some back exercises work the rear delts, they may not be enough to provide a significant amount of stimulation. Therefore, it's essential to include exercises that isolate the rear delts in your workout routine.

Here are some exercises that work your rear delts:

  • Rear delt flyes
  • Reverse pec deck flyes
  • Bent-over lateral raises
  • Face pulls

Step by Step

For each exercise mentioned above, here's a quick step-by-step guide on how to perform them:

Rear Delt Flyes

  1. Grab a set of dumbbells and stand with your feet shoulder-width apart.
  2. Bend your knees slightly and hinge forward at your hips, keeping your back straight.
  3. Bring your arms out to your sides, keeping your elbows slightly bent.
  4. Squeeze your shoulder blades together as you lift the weights up and out to the side.
  5. Pause for a second, then slowly lower the weights back down to the starting position.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of reps.

Reverse Pec Deck Flyes

  1. Adjust the seat of the pec deck machine so that the handles are at shoulder height.
  2. Sit with your back against the pad and grab the handles with an overhand grip.
  3. Keep your elbows slightly bent and squeeze your shoulder blades together as you bring the handles back towards your chest.
  4. Pause for a second, then slowly release back to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.

Bent-Over Lateral Raises

  1. Grab a set of dumbbells and stand with your feet shoulder-width apart.
  2. Bend your knees slightly and hinge forward at your hips, keeping your back straight.
  3. Let the dumbbells hang down in front of you with your palms facing each other.
  4. Keeping your elbows slightly bent, lift the weights out to your sides until your arms are parallel to the ground.
  5. Pause for a second, then slowly lower the weights back down to the starting position.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of reps.

Face Pulls

  1. Attach a rope to a cable machine at chest height.
  2. Grab the rope with both hands and step back a few feet.
  3. Sit back slightly and keep your arms extended in front of you.
  4. Pull the rope towards your face, keeping your elbows high and your hands at eye level.
  5. Pause for a second, then slowly release back to the starting position.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of reps.

Tips

When performing exercises that target your rear delts, it's important to keep a few things in mind:

  • Use a weight that is challenging but allows you to maintain proper form.
  • Perform each exercise with a slow and controlled motion.
  • Focus on squeezing your rear delts at the top of each movement.
  • Incorporate rear delt exercises into your workout routine at least once a week.

Solution

So, do back exercises work rear delts? The answer is yes and no. While some back exercises do work the rear delts, they may not provide enough stimulation to promote significant growth. Therefore, it's essential to include exercises that specifically target the rear delts in your workout routine.

By incorporating exercises like rear delt flyes, reverse pec deck flyes, bent-over lateral raises, and face pulls, you can effectively work your rear delts and promote growth. So, if you're looking to build a strong and defined shoulder, don't neglect your rear delts.

FAQs

Q: Can you work your rear delts without weights?

A: Yes, you can work your rear delts without weights. Exercises like band pull-aparts and bodyweight rows can effectively target your rear delts.

Q: How often should I work my rear delts?

A: It's recommended to work your rear delts at least once a week. You can incorporate exercises that target your rear delts into your back or shoulder workout routine.

Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • Isolate the rear delts for maximum growth
  • Improve shoulder strength and stability
  • Enhance overall shoulder definition

Cons:

  • May not be enough to promote significant growth if not done consistently
  • Can cause shoulder pain if performed with improper form or too much weight
  • May require additional equipment or gym membership to perform certain exercises
