If you own an Apple Watch, you might be wondering how it calculates move calories. Move calories are the calories you burn while moving, and they are different from your resting calories. Understanding how your Apple Watch calculates move calories can help you better track your fitness goals and make informed decisions about your workouts.

How to Check Your Move Calories on Apple Watch

Before we dive into how Apple Watch calculates move calories, let's first understand how to check your move calories on the device:

  1. Open the Activity app on your Apple Watch
  2. Swipe left to view your Move ring
  3. You'll see the total number of calories you've burned for the day, including your resting calories and move calories

Now that you know how to check your move calories, let's take a closer look at how Apple Watch calculates them.

Step-by-Step Guide on How Apple Watch Calculates Move Calories

According to Apple, your Apple Watch calculates move calories using the following steps:

  1. Your watch tracks your movement using its accelerometer and gyroscope sensors
  2. It then uses an algorithm to determine the intensity of your movement
  3. The watch compares your movement to the intensity of your previous activity and calculates the number of calories you burn based on this comparison

When calculating move calories, your Apple Watch takes into account your height, weight, age, and gender. It also considers the type of activity you are doing, such as running, walking, or cycling.

It's worth noting that your Apple Watch doesn't track every single movement you make throughout the day. Instead, it focuses on activities that get your heart rate up and that you do for at least one minute at a time.

Tips on How to Burn More Move Calories

If you want to burn more move calories, here are some tips:

  • Engage in activities that get your heart rate up, such as running, swimming, or dancing
  • Choose activities that you enjoy, as you're more likely to stick with them
  • Set goals in the Activity app and try to beat them each day
  • Take breaks throughout the day to stand up and move around
  • Use the Breathe app on your Apple Watch to reduce stress, which can help you burn more calories

Solutions to Common Issues with Apple Watch Move Calories

If you're experiencing issues with your Apple Watch's move calories, here are some solutions:

  • Make sure your Apple Watch is snug on your wrist and that the sensors are in contact with your skin
  • Update your Apple Watch to the latest software version
  • Restart your Apple Watch
  • Reset your calibration data by going to Settings > Privacy > Motion & Fitness > Reset Calibration Data

FAQs about Apple Watch Move Calories

Q: Does Apple Watch count steps?

A: Yes, Apple Watch counts steps using its accelerometer sensor.

Q: How does Apple Watch calculate resting calories?

A: Apple Watch calculates resting calories based on your basal metabolic rate (BMR), which is the number of calories your body burns at rest.

Q: Can I adjust my move goal?

A: Yes, you can adjust your move goal by going to the Activity app on your Apple Watch or iPhone.

Pros and Cons of Apple Watch Move Calories

Pros

  • Apple Watch provides an easy way to track your move calories
  • The watch takes into account your height, weight, age, and gender when calculating move calories
  • You can set goals and track your progress over time

Cons

  • Your Apple Watch may not track every single movement you make throughout the day
  • The watch's sensors may not be accurate for certain activities, such as weightlifting
  • Some users may find the move goals to be too easy or too difficult to achieve

Overall, understanding how Apple Watch calculates move calories can help you better track your fitness goals and make informed decisions about your workouts. By following the tips outlined in this article, you can burn more move calories and achieve your fitness goals faster.
