How Many Exercises Should a Beginner Do? | Tips, Solution, FAQ, Pros and Cons

As a beginner in the world of fitness, it can be overwhelming to figure out how many exercises you should be doing. There's a lot of conflicting information out there, and it can be hard to know what's right for you. In this article, we'll break down everything you need to know about how many exercises a beginner should do, including tips, solutions, FAQs, pros, and cons.

How Many Exercises Should a Beginner Do?

The answer to this question will depend on a few factors, including your fitness goals, your fitness level, and how much time you have to devote to working out. As a general rule of thumb, beginners should aim to do two to three exercises per muscle group per workout, and aim to work out each muscle group two to three times a week.

For example, if you're doing a chest workout, you might do three exercises targeting your chest muscles, such as bench press, dumbbell flys, and push-ups. If you're doing a full-body workout, you might do two to three exercises targeting each muscle group, such as squats, lunges, and leg presses for your legs, and pull-ups, rows, and lat pulldowns for your back.

It's important to remember that quality is more important than quantity. If you're just starting out, it's better to do a few exercises with proper form and good intensity than to try to do too much and risk injury or burnout.

How to Determine the Right Number of Exercises for You

The right number of exercises for you will depend on your individual fitness goals, your fitness level, and your schedule. Here are some tips to help you determine the right number of exercises:

  • Start with a basic workout plan: If you're new to working out, start with a basic workout plan that includes two to three exercises per muscle group. Over time, you can add more exercises as you become more comfortable and confident.
  • Consider your fitness goals: If your goal is to build muscle, you may need to do more exercises per muscle group than if your goal is to lose weight or improve your overall fitness.
  • Listen to your body: If you're feeling fatigued or sore, it may be a sign that you're doing too much. It's important to listen to your body and adjust your workout plan accordingly.
  • Consult a professional: If you're unsure about how many exercises you should be doing, consider consulting a personal trainer or fitness professional who can help you create a customized workout plan based on your goals and fitness level.

Step-by-Step Guide to Creating a Workout Plan

Creating a workout plan can be intimidating, especially if you're new to fitness. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you create a workout plan that works for you:

  1. Identify your fitness goals: What do you want to achieve through exercise? Do you want to lose weight, build muscle, improve your overall fitness, or something else?
  2. Determine your fitness level: Are you a beginner, intermediate, or advanced exerciser? This will help you determine how many exercises you should be doing and at what intensity.
  3. Decide on your workout frequency: How many days a week do you want to work out? This will help you determine how many exercises you should be doing per muscle group per workout.
  4. Choose your exercises: Choose two to three exercises per muscle group that you enjoy and that align with your fitness goals. Make sure to include exercises that target all major muscle groups.
  5. Create your workout plan: Use a workout planner or app to create a plan that includes the exercises you've chosen, the number of sets and reps for each exercise, and how many days a week you'll be working out.
  6. Stick to your plan: Consistency is key when it comes to seeing results from exercise. Stick to your plan as closely as possible, but don't be afraid to make adjustments if needed.

Tips for Beginners Starting a Fitness Journey

Starting a fitness journey can be challenging, but these tips can help make the process a little easier:

  • Start slow: Don't try to do too much too soon. Start with a basic workout plan and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts over time.
  • Find activities you enjoy: Exercise doesn't have to be a chore. Find activities you enjoy, whether it's dancing, hiking, or playing a sport.
  • Set realistic goals: Don't set yourself up for failure by setting unrealistic goals. Set small, achievable goals and celebrate your progress along the way.
  • Get support: Having a workout buddy or joining a fitness community can help keep you motivated and accountable.
  • Rest and recover: Rest and recovery are just as important as exercise. Make sure to give your body time to rest and recover between workouts.

Solutions to Common Beginner Workout Problems

As a beginner, you may run into some common workout problems. Here are some solutions to help you overcome them:

  • Problem: Lack of motivation. Solution: Find an accountability partner or try a new workout that excites you.
  • Problem: Muscle soreness. Solution: Take rest days and do gentle stretching or foam rolling to help alleviate soreness.
  • Problem: Boredom. Solution: Mix up your workouts by trying new exercises or activities.
  • Problem: Lack of time. Solution: Try shorter, more intense workouts or break up your workouts throughout the day.

FAQs About How Many Exercises a Beginner Should Do

Here are some frequently asked questions about how many exercises a beginner should do:

  • Q: Is it better to do more exercises or fewer exercises as a beginner?
  • A: It's generally better to do fewer exercises with proper form and good intensity than to try to do too much and risk injury or burnout.
  • Q: How many exercises should I do per muscle group as a beginner?
  • A: Beginners should aim to do two to three exercises per muscle group per workout.
  • Q: How often should I work out as a beginner?
  • A: Beginners should aim to work out each muscle group two to three times a week.

Pros and Cons of Doing More or Fewer Exercises

Here are some pros and cons of doing more or fewer exercises:

Pros of doing more exercises:

  • You may see faster results.
  • You may feel more accomplished after a workout.
  • You may enjoy the variety of exercises.

Cons of doing more exercises:

  • You may risk injury or burnout if you do too much too soon.
  • You may not have enough time to complete all of the exercises.
  • You may not be able to maintain proper form and intensity if you're doing too many exercises.

Pros of doing fewer exercises:

  • You may be able to maintain proper form and intensity for each exercise.
  • You may avoid injury and burnout.
  • You may have more time to focus on other aspects of your life.

Cons of doing fewer exercises:

  • You may not see results as quickly.
  • You may feel less accomplished after a workout.
  • You may get bored with the same exercises over time.

Ultimately, the right number of exercises for you will depend on your individual fitness goals, your fitness level, and your schedule. By following the tips and guidelines in this article, you can create a workout plan that works for you and helps you achieve your fitness goals.
