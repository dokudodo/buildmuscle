What is Anaerobic Exercise?

Before we dive into how many minutes of anaerobic exercise you should be doing per week, let's first define what anaerobic exercise is. Anaerobic exercise is a type of exercise that is short in duration but high in intensity. It typically involves activities that require quick bursts of energy, such as lifting weights, sprinting, or jumping.

Unlike aerobic exercise, which requires oxygen to produce energy, anaerobic exercise relies on energy sources stored in the muscles themselves. This means that anaerobic exercise can only be sustained for short periods of time before fatigue sets in.

How Many Minutes of Anaerobic Exercise per Week?

The American College of Sports Medicine recommends that adults perform at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic exercise per week. However, when it comes to anaerobic exercise, there are no specific guidelines on how many minutes you should be doing per week.

That being said, incorporating some form of anaerobic exercise into your workout routine can be beneficial for improving overall fitness and athletic performance. It's recommended to aim for at least two to three days per week of anaerobic exercise, with each session lasting anywhere from 10 to 30 minutes.

How to Incorporate Anaerobic Exercise into Your Workout Routine

When incorporating anaerobic exercise into your workout routine, it's important to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration over time. Here are some examples of anaerobic exercises you can incorporate into your routine:

  • HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training)
  • Sprints
  • Jumps (such as box jumps or jump squats)
  • Weightlifting
  • Plyometrics (such as burpees or jump lunges)

You can also try incorporating circuit training into your routine, which combines both aerobic and anaerobic exercises for a full-body workout. Remember to always warm up before starting any anaerobic exercise and to cool down and stretch afterwards to prevent injury.

Tips for Getting the Most out of Your Anaerobic Workouts

Here are some tips for getting the most out of your anaerobic workouts:

  • Focus on proper form to prevent injury
  • Gradually increase the intensity and duration over time
  • Listen to your body and rest when needed
  • Mix up your workouts to prevent boredom and plateauing
  • Stay hydrated and fuel your body with proper nutrition

FAQ

What are the benefits of anaerobic exercise?

Anaerobic exercise can help improve overall fitness, increase muscle strength and endurance, and improve athletic performance. It can also help boost metabolism, leading to increased calorie burn even after your workout is over.

Is anaerobic exercise safe?

When done properly with proper form and technique, anaerobic exercise is generally safe for most people. However, it's important to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration over time to prevent injury.

Pros and Cons of Anaerobic Exercise

Pros

  • Improves overall fitness
  • Increase muscle strength and endurance
  • Improves athletic performance
  • Boosts metabolism for increased calorie burn

Cons

  • Can be high impact and lead to joint pain or injury if done improperly
  • Requires proper warm-up and cool-down to prevent injury
  • May not be suitable for individuals with certain medical conditions or injuries

Overall, incorporating anaerobic exercise into your workout routine can be a great way to improve overall fitness and athletic performance. Remember to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration over time, and always listen to your body and rest when needed.
