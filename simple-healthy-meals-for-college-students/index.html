College life can be hectic, with classes, extracurricular activities, and social events all vying for your attention. It's easy to fall into the trap of eating fast food or microwavable meals, but these options are often high in calories, sodium, and unhealthy fats. In this article, we'll provide you with some simple healthy meals for college students that are quick and easy to make, and won't break the bank.

1. Avocado Toast

Avocado toast is a trendy and healthy breakfast option that's easy to make. Simply toast a slice of whole-grain bread, mash half an avocado, and spread it on top of the toast. Sprinkle some salt and pepper, and add toppings like sliced tomatoes, a fried egg, or a drizzle of balsamic glaze. This meal is packed with healthy fats, fiber, and protein, making it a perfect choice for a busy day ahead.

Ingredients:

  • 1 slice of whole-grain bread
  • 1/2 avocado
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: sliced tomatoes, fried egg, balsamic glaze

2. Chicken Stir-Fry

A chicken stir-fry is a quick and easy dinner option that's healthy and delicious. Start by cooking some brown rice according to the package instructions. While the rice is cooking, heat some oil in a pan, and cook diced chicken breast until browned. Add sliced vegetables like bell peppers, broccoli, and carrots, and cook until tender. Add a sauce made from soy sauce, honey, ginger, and garlic, and stir until everything is coated. Serve the stir-fry over the brown rice for a complete and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup brown rice
  • 1 boneless, skinless chicken breast, diced
  • Assorted sliced vegetables (bell peppers, broccoli, carrots)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 2 cloves minced garlic

3. Quinoa Salad

A quinoa salad is a healthy and filling lunch option that can be customized to your liking. Cook some quinoa according to the package instructions, and let it cool. Add chopped vegetables like cucumbers, tomatoes, and bell peppers, as well as some crumbled feta cheese, chopped nuts, and a dressing made from olive oil, lemon juice, and Dijon mustard. This meal is packed with protein, fiber, and healthy fats, making it a great option for a long day of classes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • Assorted chopped vegetables (cucumbers, tomatoes, bell peppers)
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (walnuts, almonds, etc.)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard

4. Black Bean Tacos

Black bean tacos are a healthy and vegetarian-friendly dinner option that's easy to make. Start by heating some oil in a pan and sautÃ©ing diced onions and bell peppers. Add a can of drained and rinsed black beans, and cook until heated through. Warm up some corn tortillas, and add the black bean mixture to each one. Top with shredded cheese, salsa, and avocado slices for a delicious and filling meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 diced onion
  • 1 diced bell pepper
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • Corn tortillas
  • Shredded cheese
  • Salsa
  • Sliced avocado

5. Veggie Omelette

An omelette is a healthy and easy breakfast option that can be customized to your taste. Whisk together 2-3 eggs in a bowl, and add some chopped vegetables like tomatoes, spinach, and mushrooms. Heat some oil in a pan, and pour the egg mixture into the pan. Cook until the eggs are set, and fold the omelette in half. Serve with some whole-grain toast for a complete meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2-3 eggs
  • Assorted chopped vegetables (tomatoes, spinach, mushrooms)
  • Oil for cooking
  • Whole-grain toast

6. Fruit Smoothie

A fruit smoothie is a healthy and refreshing breakfast or snack option that's easy to make. In a blender, combine some frozen fruit like berries, bananas, or mangoes, with some plain Greek yogurt and a splash of almond milk. Blend until smooth, and serve in a glass. This meal is packed with vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making it a great option for a busy day.

Ingredients:

  • Assorted frozen fruit (berries, bananas, mangoes)
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup almond milk

7. Roasted Vegetables

Roasted vegetables are a healthy and easy side dish that can be paired with any protein. Cut up some vegetables like sweet potatoes, broccoli, and Brussels sprouts, and toss them in olive oil and seasonings like garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Roast in the oven at 400 degrees Fahrenheit for 20-30 minutes, or until tender and golden brown. Serve alongside a protein like grilled chicken or baked salmon for a complete meal.

Ingredients:

  • Assorted vegetables (sweet potatoes, broccoli, Brussels sprouts)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Assorted seasonings (garlic powder, salt, pepper)

8. Baked Salmon

Baked salmon is a healthy and delicious dinner option that's easy to make. Preheat your oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit, and line a baking sheet with foil. Season a salmon fillet with salt, pepper, and a drizzle of olive oil, and place it on the baking sheet. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through. Serve with some roasted vegetables or a side salad for a complete and healthy meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 salmon fillet
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

9. Turkey Burger

A turkey burger is a healthy and low-fat alternative to a traditional beef burger. Mix together some ground turkey, diced onions, and seasonings like garlic powder and paprika. Form into patties, and cook on a grill or in a pan until browned and cooked through. Serve on a whole-grain bun with some lettuce, tomato, and avocado for a delicious and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1/2 diced onion
  • Assorted seasonings (garlic powder, paprika, etc.)
  • Whole-grain buns
