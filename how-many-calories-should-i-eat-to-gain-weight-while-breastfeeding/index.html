How Many Calories Should I Eat To Gain Weight While Breastfeeding

Are you struggling to gain weight while breastfeeding your little one? It’s a common concern among new mothers, as breastfeeding requires a lot of energy and can burn up to 500 extra calories per day. However, gaining weight while breastfeeding is possible with a proper diet and calorie intake. In this article, we’ll discuss how many calories you should eat to gain weight while breastfeeding.

How to Gain Weight While Breastfeeding

The key to gaining weight while breastfeeding is to eat more calories than you burn. This means consuming more calories than the recommended daily intake for non-breastfeeding women. However, you want to make sure you’re gaining weight in a healthy way, so it’s important to eat nutrient-dense foods and avoid junk food.

It’s also important to note that your body needs time to recover after giving birth, so it’s best to wait until your baby is at least 2 months old before actively trying to gain weight.

Step-by-Step Guide to Determine Your Calorie Needs

The number of calories you need to consume to gain weight while breastfeeding depends on several factors, including your pre-pregnancy weight, current weight, activity level, and baby’s age. Here’s a step-by-step guide to determine your calorie needs:

Step 1: Calculate your basal metabolic rate (BMR)

Your BMR is the number of calories your body burns at rest. You can use an online calculator to determine your BMR based on your age, height, weight, and gender.

Step 2: Factor in your activity level

Multiply your BMR by your activity level to determine your total daily energy expenditure (TDEE). This takes into account the calories you burn through daily activities such as walking, cleaning, and exercise.

Step 3: Add calories for breastfeeding

Add an additional 500-600 calories to your TDEE to account for the calories burned while breastfeeding. This will give you the total number of calories you need to consume each day to maintain your current weight.

Step 4: Add calories for weight gain

To gain weight, you need to consume more calories than your TDEE. Aim to consume an additional 500-1000 calories per day, depending on your weight gain goals. This will help you gain weight at a healthy rate of 1-2 pounds per week.

Tips for Eating More Calories While Breastfeeding

Here are some tips for increasing your calorie intake while breastfeeding:

  • Choose nutrient-dense foods such as lean protein, whole grains, fruits, and vegetables.
  • Eat small, frequent meals throughout the day to avoid feeling too full or uncomfortable.
  • Include healthy fats such as avocado, nuts, and olive oil in your diet.
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Avoid junk food and sugary drinks, as they provide empty calories and can be harmful to your health.

Solution for Gaining Weight While Breastfeeding

If you’re struggling to gain weight while breastfeeding, consider talking to a registered dietitian who can help you develop a personalized meal plan. They can also provide guidance on how to safely increase your calorie intake and ensure you’re getting all the nutrients you and your baby need.

FAQs

Q: Can I lose weight while breastfeeding?

A: Yes, it’s possible to lose weight while breastfeeding. However, it’s important to do so in a healthy way and not restrict your calorie intake too much, as this can affect your milk supply.

Q: How much weight should I aim to gain while breastfeeding?

A: The amount of weight you should aim to gain while breastfeeding depends on your pre-pregnancy weight and current weight. A general guideline is to aim for a weight gain of 25-35 pounds during pregnancy and an additional 1-2 pounds per week while breastfeeding.

Pros and Cons of Gaining Weight While Breastfeeding

Pros:

  • Gaining weight while breastfeeding can help you recover from childbirth and provide energy for breastfeeding.
  • It can also help you maintain a healthy weight and prevent postpartum depression.

Cons:

  • Gaining too much weight can increase your risk of gestational diabetes, high blood pressure, and other pregnancy complications.
  • It can also be difficult to lose the weight after you stop breastfeeding.

In conclusion, gaining weight while breastfeeding is possible with a proper diet and calorie intake. Use the step-by-step guide above to determine your calorie needs and aim to consume nutrient-dense foods to gain weight in a healthy way. Consider talking to a registered dietitian for personalized guidance. Remember to prioritize your health and the health of your baby.
