How Many Calories Do You Burn a Day Not Exercising?

When you're not exercising, your body continues to burn calories to keep your vital organs functioning properly. This process is known as your basal metabolic rate (BMR) and it accounts for the majority of the calories you burn each day. On average, an adult's BMR accounts for about 60-75% of their total daily energy expenditure.

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

Your BMR is the number of calories your body burns at rest to keep your essential bodily functions going, such as breathing, circulating blood, and maintaining body temperature. BMR is influenced by various factors, including age, sex, weight, and height.

Factors That Affect BMR

Some of the factors that can affect your BMR include:

  • Age: As you age, your BMR slows down.
  • Sex: Men generally have a higher BMR than women due to having more muscle mass.
  • Weight: The more you weigh, the higher your BMR will be.
  • Height: Taller people tend to have a higher BMR.
  • Body composition: If you have more muscle mass, your BMR will be higher.

Calories Burned During Sleep

When you're sleeping, your body is still burning calories to keep your vital functions going. The number of calories you burn during sleep varies depending on your BMR and the length of your sleep. On average, a person burns around 50-100 calories per hour of sleep.

Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis (NEAT)

NEAT refers to the calories you burn through daily activities such as walking, cleaning, and fidgeting. This can account for up to 15-50% of your total daily energy expenditure, depending on your activity level.

Calories Burned Through Diet

Your body also burns calories through the process of digesting and metabolizing food. This is known as the thermic effect of food (TEF) and accounts for about 10% of your total daily energy expenditure.

Tips to Burn More Calories

If you're looking to burn more calories throughout the day, here are some tips:

  • Incorporate more movement into your daily routine, such as taking the stairs instead of the elevator.
  • Stand up and move around every hour if you have a sedentary job.
  • Lift weights to increase muscle mass, which will increase your BMR.
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes protein, fiber, and healthy fats to support your BMR.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many calories do I burn a day without doing anything?

On average, an adult burns between 1,000-2,000 calories per day without any physical activity.

How do I calculate my basal metabolic rate?

There are various online calculators that can help you determine your BMR based on your age, sex, weight, and height.

Can I increase my BMR?

Yes, you can increase your BMR through strength training, increasing your muscle mass, and eating a balanced diet.

Conclusion

Now you know how many calories you burn a day not exercising and the factors that can affect your BMR. Remember, even when you're not working out, your body is still burning calories to keep your essential functions going. By incorporating more movement into your daily routine and supporting your BMR through diet and exercise, you can increase your overall calorie burn and support a healthy lifestyle.
