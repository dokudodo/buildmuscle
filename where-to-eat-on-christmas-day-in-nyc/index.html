Are you in New York City during the holiday season and wondering where to eat on Christmas Day? Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll explore some of the best places to eat on Christmas Day in NYC. From traditional Christmas fare to international cuisine, there are plenty of options to choose from. Traditional Christmas Fare

If you're looking for a traditional Christmas meal, there are plenty of options in NYC. Some popular choices include:

  • The Boathouse in Central Park: This iconic restaurant serves a prix-fixe Christmas menu with dishes like roasted turkey, glazed ham, and pumpkin pie.
  • The Capital Grille: This steakhouse offers a traditional Christmas menu with options like slow-roasted turkey, mashed potatoes, and cranberry sauce.
  • The Russian Tea Room: This historic restaurant serves a three-course Christmas menu with traditional dishes like borscht, beef stroganoff, and honey cake.

International Cuisine

If you're in the mood for something different, there are plenty of restaurants in NYC that serve international cuisine. Some options include:

  • Buddakan: This trendy restaurant serves an Asian-inspired Christmas menu with dishes like Peking duck, dim sum, and spicy Szechuan beef.
  • Casa Mono: This Spanish restaurant offers a Christmas menu with dishes like paella, roasted lamb, and churros with chocolate sauce.
  • Ilili: This Lebanese restaurant serves a Christmas menu with dishes like lamb kebabs, falafel, and baklava.

Vegetarian and Vegan Options

If you're a vegetarian or vegan, there are plenty of options in NYC for you as well. Some popular choices include:

  • Candle 79: This vegan restaurant serves a Christmas menu with dishes like seitan piccata, stuffed acorn squash, and chocolate mousse cake.
  • Blossom: This vegetarian restaurant offers a Christmas menu with options like roasted root vegetables, mushroom risotto, and pumpkin cheesecake.
  • Hangawi: This Korean restaurant serves a vegan Christmas menu with dishes like mushroom hot pot, stir-fried vegetables, and pumpkin porridge.

Family-Friendly Restaurants

If you're dining with kids, you'll want to find a restaurant that's family-friendly. Some options include:

  • Hard Rock Cafe: This rock-and-roll-themed restaurant offers a Christmas menu with options like burgers, mac and cheese, and brownie sundaes.
  • Serendipity 3: This whimsical restaurant serves a Christmas menu with dishes like grilled cheese sandwiches, hot dogs, and frozen hot chocolate.
  • Max Brenner: This chocolate-themed restaurant serves a Christmas menu with options like waffles, crepes, and fondue.

Romantic Restaurants

If you're looking for a romantic dinner on Christmas Day, there are plenty of options in NYC. Some choices include:

  • One if by Land, Two if by Sea: This upscale restaurant is housed in a historic carriage house and serves a Christmas menu with dishes like lobster bisque, filet mignon, and chocolate cake.
  • Le Bernardin: This Michelin-starred restaurant serves a Christmas menu with options like caviar, sea urchin, and foie gras.
  • Gramercy Tavern: This elegant restaurant serves a Christmas menu with dishes like roasted duck, scallops, and apple tart.

Making Reservations

If you're planning to dine out on Christmas Day, it's important to make a reservation well in advance. Many restaurants fill up quickly, so don't wait until the last minute. You can make reservations online or by calling the restaurant directly. Be sure to confirm your reservation a few days before Christmas to avoid any mix-ups.

Tips for Dining Out on Christmas Day

Dining out on Christmas Day can be a wonderful experience, but it's important to keep a few things in mind:

  • Make reservations well in advance.
  • Be prepared for limited menu options.
  • Expect higher prices than usual.
  • Be patient with the staff, who are working on a holiday.
  • Don't forget to tip generously.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I find restaurants open on Christmas Day in NYC?

Yes, there are many restaurants open on Christmas Day in NYC. However, it's important to make a reservation well in advance.

Do I need to dress up to dine out on Christmas Day?

It depends on the restaurant. Some restaurants have dress codes, while others are more casual. Be sure to check the restaurant's website or call ahead to find out their dress code.

Are there any restaurants that offer Christmas brunch?

Yes, many restaurants offer Christmas brunch. Some popular choices include Balthazar, Sarabeth's, and The Smith.

Pros and Cons of Eating Out on Christmas Day

Pros

  • You don't have to cook or clean up.
  • You can try new restaurants or cuisines.
  • You can spend time with family and friends without the stress of hosting.

Cons

  • Restaurants may have limited menu options.
  • Prices may be higher than usual.
  • Restaurants may be crowded and noisy.

Conclusion

If you're wondering where to eat on Christmas Day in NYC, there are plenty of options to choose from. Whether you're looking for traditional Christmas fare or international cuisine, there's something for everyone. Just be sure to make your reservations well in advance and be prepared for higher prices and limited menu options. Happy holidays!
