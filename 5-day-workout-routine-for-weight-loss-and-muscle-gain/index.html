Looking to lose weight and gain muscle? It can be a daunting task to figure out where to start, but a structured workout routine can be the key to success. In this article, we will provide you with a 5 day workout routine that will help you achieve your weight loss and muscle gain goals.

Table of Contents

  • Day 2: Back and Biceps
  • Day 3: Legs
  • Day 4: Shoulders
  • Day 5: Full Body

  • FAQs

Day 1: Chest and Triceps

On day 1, you will focus on exercises that target your chest and triceps. This will include exercises such as bench press, push-ups, tricep dips, and tricep extensions. Be sure to warm up properly before beginning your workout, and aim for 3-4 sets of 10-12 reps for each exercise.

Bench Press

One of the most popular chest exercises, the bench press targets your chest muscles and triceps. Be sure to use proper form and control throughout the exercise to avoid injury. Start with a weight that is comfortable for you and gradually increase as you build strength.

Push-Ups

A classic bodyweight exercise, push-ups are a great way to target your chest and triceps. If you are new to push-ups, start with modified push-ups on your knees and gradually work your way up to full push-ups.

Tricep Dips

Tricep dips are a great exercise for targeting your triceps. You can perform dips on a bench or chair, or using dip bars if available. Be sure to keep your elbows close to your body and avoid locking out your elbows at the top of the movement.

Tricep Extensions

Tricep extensions can be performed using dumbbells or a cable machine. Be sure to keep your elbows close to your head and avoid swinging your arms during the exercise.

Day 2: Back and Biceps

On day 2, you will focus on exercises that target your back and biceps. This will include exercises such as pull-ups, lat pulldowns, bicep curls, and hammer curls. Again, be sure to warm up properly before beginning your workout, and aim for 3-4 sets of 10-12 reps for each exercise.

Pull-Ups

Pull-ups are a great exercise for targeting your back and biceps. If you are unable to perform a full pull-up, start with assisted pull-ups using a machine or resistance band.

Lat Pulldowns

Lat pulldowns are another great exercise for targeting your back. Be sure to use proper form and control throughout the exercise, and avoid using momentum to pull the weight down.

Bicep Curls

Bicep curls can be performed using dumbbells or a barbell. Be sure to keep your elbows close to your body and avoid swinging your arms during the exercise.

Hammer Curls

Hammer curls are a variation of bicep curls that target your biceps and forearms. Be sure to keep your elbows close to your body and avoid swinging your arms during the exercise.

Day 3: Legs

On day 3, you will focus on exercises that target your legs. This will include exercises such as squats, lunges, leg press, and calf raises. Once again, be sure to warm up properly before beginning your workout, and aim for 3-4 sets of 10-12 reps for each exercise.

Squats

Squats are a great exercise for targeting your quads, hamstrings, and glutes. Be sure to use proper form and control throughout the exercise, and avoid locking out your knees at the top of the movement.

Lunges

Lunges are another great exercise for targeting your legs. You can perform walking lunges or stationary lunges, and can use dumbbells or a barbell for added resistance.

Leg Press

The leg press machine is a great way to target your quads, hamstrings, and glutes. Be sure to adjust the seat and foot position to fit your body and avoid locking out your knees at the top of the movement.

Calf Raises

Calf raises can be performed using a machine or with dumbbells. Be sure to use proper form and control throughout the exercise, and avoid bouncing at the bottom of the movement.

Day 4: Shoulders

On day 4, you will focus on exercises that target your shoulders. This will include exercises such as overhead press, lateral raises, front raises, and upright rows. As always, be sure to warm up properly before beginning your workout, and aim for 3-4 sets of 10-12 reps for each exercise.

Overhead Press

The overhead press targets your shoulders and can be performed using dumbbells or a barbell. Be sure to use proper form and control throughout the exercise, and avoid arching your back or leaning forward.

Lateral Raises

Lateral raises target your side delts and can be performed using dumbbells or a cable machine. Be sure to use proper form and control throughout the exercise, and avoid swinging your arms or using momentum to lift the weight.

Front Raises

Front raises target your front delts and can be performed using dumbbells or a cable machine. Be sure to use proper form and control throughout the exercise, and avoid swinging your arms or using momentum to lift the weight.

Upright Rows

Upright rows target your upper traps and can be performed using dumbbells or a barbell. Be sure to use proper form and control throughout the exercise, and avoid pulling the weight too high or too close to your body.

Day 5: Full Body

On day 5, you will perform a full body workout that incorporates exercises from each of the previous days. This will help you to maintain balance and ensure that you are targeting all of your muscle groups. Be sure to warm up properly before beginning your workout, and aim for 3-4 sets of 10-12 reps for each exercise.

Sample Full Body Workout:

  • Bench Press
  • Pull-Ups
  • Squats
  • Overhead Press
  • Lateral Raises
  • Bicep Curls
  • Tricep Dips
  • Calf Raises

Tips for Success

Here are some tips to help you succeed with your weight loss and muscle gain goals:

  • Be consistent with your workouts, aiming for at least 5 days per week
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, complex carbohydrates, and healthy fats
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water throughout the day
  • Get enough rest and recovery time to allow your muscles to repair and grow
  • Track your progress and make adjustments to your routine as needed

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about the 5 day workout routine for weight loss and muscle gain:

How long should I rest between sets?

You should aim for 1-2 minutes of rest between sets to allow your muscles to recover and prepare for the next set.

Can I do cardio on the same days as my weightlifting workouts?

Yes, you can do cardio on the same days as your weightlifting workouts, but be sure to prioritize your weightlifting exercises and avoid overtraining.

How long will it take to see results?

Results will vary depending on your starting point and the effort you put into your workouts and diet. With consistency and dedication, you can expect to see results within a few weeks to a few months.
