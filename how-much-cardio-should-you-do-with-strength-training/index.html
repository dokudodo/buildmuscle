How Much Cardio Should You Do With Strength Training

When it comes to fitness, many people wonder how much cardio they should do with strength training. There is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question as everyone’s fitness goals and fitness levels are different. However, in this article, we will explore this topic in-depth and provide you with some valuable insight.

What is Cardio?

Cardio, short for cardiovascular exercise, is any exercise that increases your heart rate and breathing rate. It is also known as aerobic exercise. Examples of cardio exercises include running, swimming, cycling, and jumping jacks.

What is Strength Training?

Strength training, also known as resistance training, is any exercise that involves using weights or resistance to build strength and muscle. Examples of strength training exercises include weightlifting, push-ups, and squats.

How Much Cardio Should You Do with Strength Training?

The amount of cardio you should do with strength training depends on your fitness goals. If you want to improve your cardiovascular endurance, then you should do more cardio. If you want to build muscle and strength, then you should focus more on strength training.

As a general rule of thumb, it is recommended that you do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio per week. This can be divided into 30-minute sessions five times a week for moderate-intensity cardio or 25-minute sessions three times a week for vigorous-intensity cardio.

For strength training, it is recommended that you do at least two to three sessions per week, focusing on all major muscle groups. These sessions should last for at least 30 minutes each.

How to Incorporate Cardio with Strength Training?

One way to incorporate cardio with strength training is to do circuit training. This involves doing a series of strength training exercises with little to no rest in between. This will keep your heart rate up and provide a cardiovascular workout as well.

You can also alternate between strength training and cardio days. For example, you can do strength training on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday, and cardio on Tuesday and Thursday.

Tips for Cardio and Strength Training

  • Warm up before starting your workout to prevent injury.
  • Start with lighter weights and work your way up gradually.
  • Make sure to use proper form when doing strength training exercises.
  • Stay hydrated during your workout.
  • Listen to your body and take rest days when needed.

FAQ

Q: Can cardio interfere with muscle growth?

A: If you do too much cardio, it can interfere with muscle growth. However, if you do the right amount of cardio, it can actually improve muscle growth by increasing blood flow and oxygen to the muscles.

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after strength training?

A: It is recommended to do cardio after strength training as your body will have already used up its glycogen stores during strength training, and will therefore be forced to burn fat for energy during cardio.

Pros and Cons of Cardio and Strength Training

Pros of Cardio

  • Improves cardiovascular endurance
  • Burns calories and aids in weight loss
  • Reduces the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, and obesity

Cons of Cardio

  • Can interfere with muscle growth if done excessively
  • High-impact cardio exercises can be hard on the joints

Pros of Strength Training

  • Builds muscle and strength
  • Increases bone density
  • Boosts metabolism and aids in weight loss

Cons of Strength Training

  • Can be intimidating for beginners
  • Requires equipment or gym membership
  • Can be hard on the muscles and cause soreness

In conclusion, how much cardio you should do with strength training depends on your fitness goals. It is important to find the right balance between cardio and strength training to achieve optimal results. By incorporating both types of exercise into your fitness routine, you can improve your overall health and fitness.
