What is Daily 5?

Daily 5 is a reading program that was developed by two teachers, Gail Boushey and Joan Moser. The program is designed to help students become independent readers by teaching them how to choose books, read independently, and work on reading comprehension.

How does Daily 5 work?

The Daily 5 program is divided into five activities:

  1. Read to Self
  2. Read to Someone
  3. Listen to Reading
  4. Word Work
  5. Work on Writing

During each activity, students work independently or with a partner for a set amount of time. The goal is for students to build their stamina for reading and become more confident readers. Teachers provide support and guidance as needed, but the focus is on student independence.

Benefits of Daily 5

There are many benefits to using the Daily 5 program in your classroom. Here are just a few:

  • Students become more confident and independent readers.
  • Students learn how to choose books that are appropriate for their reading level.
  • Students develop their reading comprehension skills.
  • The program is flexible and can be adapted to fit the needs of your students.

Concerns About Daily 5

While there are many benefits to using the Daily 5 program, there are also some concerns that teachers have raised. Here are a few:

  • The program can be difficult to implement if students are not used to working independently.
  • Some students may struggle with choosing appropriate books.
  • Teachers may need to provide additional support and guidance to some students.

Implementing Daily 5

If you are interested in implementing the Daily 5 program in your classroom, here are some steps you can take:

  1. Read the book "The Daily 5: Fostering Literacy Independence in the Elementary Grades" by Gail Boushey and Joan Moser.
  2. Introduce the program to your students and explain how it works.
  3. Model each of the five activities and provide students with plenty of practice.
  4. Gradually increase the amount of time students spend on each activity.
  5. Provide support and guidance as needed.

Tips for Using Daily 5

Here are some tips to help you make the most of the Daily 5 program:

  • Start small and gradually build up to longer periods of time.
  • Provide students with a variety of books to choose from.
  • Monitor student progress and adjust the program as needed.
  • Encourage students to share their reading experiences with each other.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about the Daily 5 program:

  • Q: Can the Daily 5 program be used in middle or high school classrooms?
  • A: Yes, the program can be adapted to fit the needs of older students.
  • Q: How long should each activity last?
  • A: The length of each activity will depend on the age and skill level of your students.
  • Q: Do students always have to work independently?
  • A: No, students can also work with a partner during the "Read to Someone" activity.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the Daily 5 program can be an effective way to help students become independent readers. While there are some concerns about the program, it can be adapted to fit the needs of your students. If you are interested in implementing the Daily 5 program in your classroom, be sure to read the book and provide plenty of support and guidance to your students.
