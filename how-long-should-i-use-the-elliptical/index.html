How Long Should I Use The Elliptical

Welcome to our guide on how long you should use the elliptical for optimum results. Elliptical machines are a popular choice for cardio workouts at home or at the gym. They are low-impact, easy to use, and offer a full-body workout. However, if you’re new to elliptical training, you may be wondering how long you need to use the machine to achieve your fitness goals. In this guide, we’ll discuss everything you need to know about the duration of your elliptical workouts.

How to Use the Elliptical

Before we dive into the duration of your elliptical workouts, let’s first discuss how to use the machine properly. Here are the basic steps:

Step 1: Adjust the Resistance

Most elliptical machines have resistance settings, which can be adjusted to increase or decrease the intensity of your workout. Start with a lower resistance level if you’re new to elliptical training.

Step 2: Get on the Machine

Step onto the pedals of the machine and grip the handles. Make sure your feet are flat on the pedals and your hands are positioned comfortably on the handles.

Step 3: Begin Pedaling

Start pedaling the machine by pushing down on the pedals with your feet. Move your arms back and forth in sync with your legs. Try to maintain a steady pace throughout your workout.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know how to use the machine, here’s a step-by-step guide to help you get the most out of your elliptical workouts:

Step 1: Warm Up

Start your workout with a 5-10 minute warm-up to get your heart rate up and prepare your muscles for exercise. Use a lower resistance level during this phase.

Step 2: Increase the Resistance

As you progress through your workout, gradually increase the resistance level to challenge your muscles and increase your heart rate.

Step 3: Maintain a Steady Pace

Try to maintain a steady pace throughout your workout, rather than starting and stopping frequently. This will help you burn more calories and improve your cardio fitness.

Step 4: Cool Down

End your workout with a 5-10 minute cool-down at a lower resistance level. This will help your heart rate return to normal and prevent muscle soreness.

Tips for Using the Elliptical

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your elliptical workouts:

  • Use proper form by keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
  • Breathe deeply and evenly throughout your workout.
  • Wear comfortable workout clothes and supportive shoes.
  • Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout.
  • Listen to your body and adjust the resistance level as needed.

Elliptical Workout Duration

Now let’s get to the main question: how long should you use the elliptical for maximum benefits? The answer depends on your fitness goals and current fitness level.

If you’re new to elliptical training, start with 20-30 minutes per session, 3-4 times per week. Gradually increase the duration and frequency of your workouts as your fitness level improves.

If your goal is to lose weight, aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity elliptical training 5 times per week. If you have limited time, you can increase the intensity of your workouts by using higher resistance levels or incorporating intervals of high-intensity training.

If your goal is to improve your cardio fitness, aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise per week, including elliptical training. You can break this up into shorter sessions throughout the week.

FAQs

Q: Can I use the elliptical every day?

A: Yes, you can use the elliptical every day, but it’s important to vary your workouts and give your muscles time to rest and recover.

Q: How many calories can I burn on the elliptical?

A: The number of calories you burn on the elliptical depends on your weight, age, and intensity level. On average, a 150-pound person can burn around 300-400 calories in 30 minutes of moderate-intensity elliptical training.

Pros and Cons of Using an Elliptical

Pros:

  • Low-impact exercise that’s easy on the joints
  • Full-body workout that targets multiple muscle groups
  • Adjustable resistance levels for a customized workout
  • Can be used at home or at the gym

Cons:

  • May not provide enough variety for some exercisers
  • Can be expensive to purchase for home use
  • May not be suitable for those with certain injuries or health conditions

In conclusion, the duration of your elliptical workouts depends on your fitness goals and current fitness level. Start with 20-30 minutes per session, 3-4 times per week, and gradually increase the duration and frequency of your workouts as your fitness level improves. Remember to use proper form, vary your workouts, and listen to your body for maximum benefits.
