How to Perform Exercises for Upper Back Pain NHS

The following exercises for upper back pain NHS are designed to improve strength and flexibility in the upper back muscles:

1. Shoulder Blade Squeeze

This exercise targets the upper back muscles and helps improve posture. To perform this exercise:

  1. Sit or stand with your arms at your sides.
  2. Squeeze your shoulder blades together as tightly as possible.
  3. Hold for 5 seconds, then release.
  4. Repeat 10 times.

2. Seated Rows

This exercise targets the upper and middle back muscles. To perform this exercise:

  1. Sit on the floor with your legs extended in front of you.
  2. Wrap a resistance band around your feet and hold the ends of the band with your hands.
  3. Pull the band towards your chest, squeezing your shoulder blades together as you do so.
  4. Hold for 5 seconds, then release.
  5. Repeat 10 times.

3. Cat Cow Stretch

This exercise targets the upper and lower back muscles. To perform this exercise:

  1. Get down on your hands and knees, with your wrists directly under your shoulders and your knees directly under your hips.
  2. Slowly arch your back, bringing your head and tailbone towards the ceiling.
  3. Hold for 5 seconds, then release.
  4. Slowly round your back, bringing your head and tailbone towards the floor.
  5. Hold for 5 seconds, then release.
  6. Repeat 10 times.

Tips to Maximize the Benefits of Exercises for Upper Back Pain NHS

Here are some tips to maximize the benefits of exercises for upper back pain NHS:

  • Start slowly and gradually increase the intensity of the exercises.
  • Breathe deeply and slowly while performing the exercises.
  • Stretch before and after the exercises to help prevent injury.
  • Consult your doctor before starting any new exercise program.

Solution for Upper Back Pain NHS

Exercises for upper back pain NHS are an effective solution for reducing pain and improving mobility. However, if your pain persists, it is important to consult your doctor or a physiotherapist for further evaluation and treatment.

Q. How long should I perform these exercises for upper back pain NHS?

A. You should perform these exercises for 10-15 minutes, 3-4 times a week.

Q. Can these exercises be performed by people with chronic back pain?

A. These exercises can be performed by people with chronic back pain, but it is important to consult your doctor or a physiotherapist before starting any new exercise program.

Q. Can I perform these exercises if I have a back injury?

A. These exercises should not be performed if you have a back injury. It is important to consult your doctor or a physiotherapist for further evaluation and treatment.
