Are you someone who enjoys exercising on an empty stomach? If so, you're not alone. Fasted cardio has become increasingly popular in recent years, with many people claiming that it helps them burn more fat. However, it's important to refuel your body after a workout. In this article, we'll discuss the best foods to eat after fasted cardio to help you recover properly.

Why You Should Eat After Fasted Cardio

While fasted cardio can be beneficial for fat loss, it's important to remember that exercise is a form of stress on the body. When you exercise, your body uses up its glycogen stores for energy. Glycogen is a form of glucose that's stored in your muscles and liver. After your glycogen stores are depleted, your body begins to break down fat for energy.

While this may sound like a good thing, it's important to remember that your body needs nutrients to repair and recover after exercise. Eating after fasted cardio can help replenish your glycogen stores and provide your body with the nutrients it needs to recover and build muscle.

Best Foods to Eat After Fasted Cardio

The best foods to eat after fasted cardio are those that are rich in carbohydrates and protein. Carbohydrates will help replenish your glycogen stores, while protein will help repair and build muscle.

Some examples of good post-workout foods include:

  • Whole grain toast with almond butter and banana slices
  • Oatmeal with berries and nuts
  • Egg omelet with vegetables and whole grain toast
  • Grilled chicken breast with sweet potato and green beans
  • Salmon with quinoa and roasted vegetables

How Much to Eat After Fasted Cardio

The amount of food you should eat after fasted cardio depends on a variety of factors, including your body weight, activity level, and fitness goals. As a general rule, aim to eat a meal or snack that contains both carbohydrates and protein within 30 minutes to an hour after your workout.

Aim for a meal that's around 300-500 calories and contains 20-30 grams of protein and 30-50 grams of carbohydrates. If you're not hungry after your workout, try to at least have a small snack to help kickstart your recovery.

When to Eat After Fasted Cardio

The best time to eat after fasted cardio is within 30 minutes to an hour after your workout. This is when your body is most receptive to nutrients and can quickly absorb and utilize them for recovery.

If you're unable to eat immediately after your workout, try to have a snack on hand that you can eat within an hour of finishing your workout.

Tips for Eating After Fasted Cardio

Here are some tips to help you fuel your body properly after fasted cardio:

  • Plan ahead and have your post-workout meal or snack ready to go
  • Choose foods that are easy to digest and won't upset your stomach
  • Drink plenty of water to help with rehydration
  • Avoid foods that are high in fat or fiber, as these can slow down digestion
  • Consider taking a protein supplement if you're unable to eat a meal or snack right away

Foods to Avoid After Fasted Cardio

While it's important to refuel your body after fasted cardio, there are some foods you should avoid. These include:

  • Processed foods and snacks
  • Foods high in sugar
  • Foods that are high in fat or fiber
  • Alcohol

These foods can slow down digestion and prevent your body from properly absorbing the nutrients it needs to recover and build muscle.

FAQs

Q: Can I eat before fasted cardio?

A: No, the whole point of fasted cardio is to exercise on an empty stomach. Eating beforehand will negate the benefits of fasted cardio.

Q: Can I drink a protein shake after fasted cardio?

A: Yes, a protein shake can be a good option if you're unable to eat a meal or snack right away.

Q: How long after fasted cardio should I wait to eat?

A: You should aim to eat within 30 minutes to an hour after your workout.

Conclusion

Fasted cardio can be a great way to burn fat, but it's important to refuel your body after your workout. Eating a meal or snack that's rich in carbohydrates and protein within 30 minutes to an hour after your workout can help replenish your glycogen stores and provide your body with the nutrients it needs to recover and build muscle. Remember to plan ahead, choose foods that are easy to digest, and avoid high-fat, high-fiber, and high-sugar foods.
