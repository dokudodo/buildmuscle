In this article, we are going to walk you through step by step on how to determine what percentage of weight you have lost, and provide you with tips and solutions on how to continue on your weight loss journey.

How to Determine What Percentage of Weight You Have Lost

Figuring out what percentage of weight you have lost is a relatively simple calculation. You will need to know your starting weight and your current weight. Once you have those two numbers, follow the steps below:

  1. Subtract your current weight from your starting weight. For example, if your starting weight was 200 pounds and your current weight is 180 pounds, you would subtract 180 from 200. This gives you a difference of 20 pounds.
  2. Divide the difference by your starting weight. Using the example above, you would divide 20 by 200. This gives you 0.1.
  3. Multiply the result by 100. This will give you your weight loss percentage. Using the example above, you would multiply 0.1 by 100, giving you a weight loss percentage of 10%.

So, in this example, you have lost 10% of your starting weight. Congratulations!

What if You Have Gained Weight?

If you have gained weight instead of losing it, the calculation is the same. You will just end up with a positive number instead of a negative number. For example, if your starting weight was 200 pounds and your current weight is 220 pounds, you would subtract 200 from 220, giving you a difference of 20 pounds. Then, you would divide 20 by 200, giving you 0.1. Finally, you would multiply 0.1 by 100, giving you a weight gain percentage of 10%.

Tips on How to Continue Your Weight Loss Journey

Congratulations on your weight loss! Now that you know what percentage of weight you have lost, it's time to focus on maintaining your weight loss or continuing on your weight loss journey. Here are some tips to help you:

  • Continue to eat a healthy, balanced diet
  • Incorporate regular exercise into your routine
  • Set realistic goals for yourself
  • Stay hydrated
  • Get enough sleep
  • Find an accountability partner

Remember, weight loss is a journey, not a destination. Be kind to yourself and celebrate your successes, no matter how small they may seem.

Solutions for Maintaining Your Weight Loss

Maintaining your weight loss can be just as challenging as losing the weight in the first place. Here are some solutions to help you maintain your weight loss:

  • Track your food intake and exercise using a journal or app
  • Stay accountable by checking in with a friend or support group
  • Set new goals for yourself to stay motivated
  • Focus on non-scale victories, such as increased energy or improved mood
  • Don't be too hard on yourself if you slip up. Just get back on track the next day.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a healthy rate of weight loss?

A healthy rate of weight loss is typically 1-2 pounds per week. Losing weight too quickly can be unhealthy and unsustainable.

What if I hit a weight loss plateau?

Hitting a weight loss plateau is common, but it can be frustrating. Try mixing up your exercise routine or adjusting your calorie intake to jumpstart your weight loss again.

Do I need to count calories to lose weight?

Counting calories can be a helpful tool for weight loss, but it's not necessary. A focus on eating whole, nutrient-dense foods and being mindful of portion sizes can also lead to weight loss.

Pros and Cons of Measuring Your Weight Loss Percentage

Pros:

  • Provides a tangible measure of progress
  • Motivating to see progress
  • Helps you set realistic goals

Cons:

  • Can be discouraging if progress is slow
  • May lead to an unhealthy focus on the number on the scale
  • Doesn't take into account muscle gain or other body composition changes

Overall, measuring your weight loss percentage can be a helpful tool on your weight loss journey. Just remember to focus on the big picture and be kind to yourself along the way.
