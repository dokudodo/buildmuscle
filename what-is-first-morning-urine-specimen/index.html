Why is First Morning Urine Important?

First morning urine specimen is the urine sample that you provide after waking up in the morning. It is considered as the most concentrated urine sample of the day since you have not consumed any fluids for several hours. This makes it ideal for various urine tests, including pregnancy tests, drug tests, and other medical tests.

When you provide a first morning urine sample, it is easier for the medical professionals to detect any abnormalities in your urine. This is because the urine is more concentrated, and any changes in the urine, such as blood or protein, can be easily detected. Moreover, if you are taking any medications, the first morning urine sample can provide accurate results, as the medications are not diluted by the fluids you drink throughout the day.

How to Collect First Morning Urine Specimen?

Collecting a first morning urine sample is a simple process. Follow these steps to collect your urine sample:

  1. Wash your hands with soap and water.
  2. Use a clean and dry container to collect your urine sample. You can use a urine cup or a sterile container for this purpose.
  3. Before collecting the urine sample, you should clean your genital area with a wipe or a clean cloth to prevent any contaminants from entering the urine sample.
  4. Begin urinating in the toilet bowl and then move the container under your urine stream to collect the sample.
  5. Make sure to collect at least 60-80 mL of urine for the test.
  6. Tightly seal the container and label it with your name and the date of collection.
  7. Store the container in a cool place until you are ready to submit it for testing.

Tips for Collecting First Morning Urine Specimen

Here are some tips to keep in mind when collecting a first morning urine sample:

  • Avoid drinking too much water or other fluids before collecting the sample, as this can dilute the urine and affect the test results.
  • Make sure to collect the urine sample as soon as you wake up, as delaying it can affect the concentration of urine.
  • If you are taking medications, consult your doctor before collecting the urine sample to ensure that the medications do not interfere with the test results.
  • If you are a female, avoid collecting the urine sample during your menstrual cycle, as this can affect the test results.
