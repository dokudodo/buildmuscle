After giving birth, women often experience various changes in their bodies, particularly in their pelvic floor muscles. These muscles support the bladder, uterus, and rectum, and can be weakened during pregnancy and childbirth. To help prevent complications such as incontinence, prolapse, and sexual problems, it is important for women to start doing pelvic floor exercises as soon as possible. In this article, we will discuss when to start doing pelvic floor exercises after birth, how to do them, and other helpful tips.

When to Start Doing Pelvic Floor Exercises

The ideal time to start doing pelvic floor exercises after birth is as soon as possible. In some cases, women may be advised to start doing them while still in the hospital or within a few days of giving birth. However, it is important to consult with your healthcare provider before starting any exercise regimen, especially if you had a complicated delivery or a C-section.

If you had a vaginal birth without any complications, you can start doing pelvic floor exercises as soon as you feel comfortable. This may be a few days or a few weeks after giving birth, depending on your individual recovery. It is important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your exercises over time.

How to Do Pelvic Floor Exercises

Pelvic floor exercises, also known as Kegels, involve contracting and relaxing the muscles that support your pelvic organs. To do them:

  1. Find a comfortable position, such as sitting or lying down.
  2. Tighten your pelvic floor muscles as if you are trying to stop the flow of urine or gas.
  3. Hold the contraction for a few seconds, then release.
  4. Repeat the exercise several times, aiming for at least 10 repetitions per session.

It is important to note that you should not hold your breath or tense your abdomen or buttocks while doing pelvic floor exercises. Also, do not overdo it, as doing too many exercises or holding the contraction for too long can cause discomfort or even further weaken your muscles.

Step-by-Step Guide to Pelvic Floor Exercises

Here is a step-by-step guide to doing pelvic floor exercises:

  1. Start by finding the right muscles. The easiest way to do this is to try stopping the flow of urine while you are urinating. The muscles you use to do this are your pelvic floor muscles.
  2. Once you have identified your pelvic floor muscles, empty your bladder and find a comfortable position.
  3. Tighten your pelvic floor muscles and hold the contraction for a few seconds (start with 3-5 seconds).
  4. Relax your muscles for the same amount of time.
  5. Repeat the exercise for at least 10 repetitions.
  6. Do the exercises at least three times a day, gradually increasing the duration and intensity over time.

Tips for Pelvic Floor Exercises After Birth

Here are some helpful tips for doing pelvic floor exercises after giving birth:

  • Start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your exercises.
  • Do the exercises regularly, at least three times a day.
  • Try to do the exercises at the same time each day, such as after you have emptied your bladder.
  • Use a reminder system, such as setting an alarm on your phone or placing sticky notes around your home, to help you remember to do the exercises.
  • Don't be discouraged if you don't see immediate results. It may take several weeks or even months to notice an improvement in your pelvic floor strength.

FAQs About Pelvic Floor Exercises After Birth

How long should I do pelvic floor exercises after giving birth?

You should continue doing pelvic floor exercises for the rest of your life to maintain your muscle strength and prevent problems such as incontinence and prolapse. However, the frequency and intensity of your exercises may vary depending on your age, health, and lifestyle.

Can I do pelvic floor exercises during pregnancy?

Yes, you can do pelvic floor exercises during pregnancy to help prevent or reduce the severity of problems such as incontinence and prolapse. However, it is important to consult with your healthcare provider before starting any exercise regimen.

Can I do too many pelvic floor exercises?

Yes, doing too many pelvic floor exercises or holding the contraction for too long can cause discomfort or even further weaken your muscles. It is important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration over time.

Pros and Cons of Pelvic Floor Exercises After Birth

Pros

  • Help prevent complications such as incontinence, prolapse, and sexual problems.
  • Can be done anywhere, anytime, and without any equipment.
  • Can improve your overall pelvic health and quality of life.

Cons

  • May take several weeks or even months to notice an improvement in your pelvic floor strength.
  • May cause discomfort or pain if done incorrectly or too vigorously.
  • May require a reminder system or some motivation to keep up with the exercises.

In conclusion, pelvic floor exercises are important for women to do after giving birth to prevent complications and improve their pelvic health. It is best to start doing them as soon as possible, gradually increasing the intensity and duration over time. If you have any concerns or questions about pelvic floor exercises or your recovery after childbirth, consult with your healthcare provider.
