Are you looking to develop a consistent gym routine to improve your fitness and overall health? Whether you're a beginner or an experienced gym-goer, having a structured workout schedule is essential to achieving your fitness goals. In this article, we will provide you with a comprehensive 5 day gym workout schedule with pictures to help you get started.

Day 1 - Chest and Triceps

On day 1, we will focus on building strength and muscle in the chest and triceps. Here is a sample workout schedule:

Warm-up

5-10 minutes of cardio (treadmill or stationary bike)

Workout

  • Bench Press (4 sets x 8-12 reps)
  • Incline Dumbbell Flyes (3 sets x 10-12 reps)
  • Cable Flyes (3 sets x 10-12 reps)
  • Tricep Dips (3 sets x 10-12 reps)
  • Tricep Pushdowns (3 sets x 10-12 reps)
  • Skull Crushers (3 sets x 10-12 reps)

Cool-down

5-10 minutes of stretching

Tips:

  • Make sure to use proper form during exercises to prevent injury.
  • Choose weights that challenge you, but allow you to complete the desired number of reps.
  • Rest for 1-2 minutes between sets.

On day 2, we will focus on building strength and muscle in the back and biceps. Here is a sample workout schedule:

Workout

Cool-down

5-10 minutes of stretching

Tips:

  • Choose weights that challenge you, but allow you to complete the desired number of reps.
  • Rest for 1-2 minutes between sets.

On day 3, we will focus on building strength and muscle in the legs and abs. Here is a sample workout schedule:

Workout

  • Leg Press (3 sets x 10-12 reps)

  • Leg Extensions (3 sets x 10-12 reps)
  • Calf Raises (3 sets x 10-12 reps)

Cool-down

5-10 minutes of stretching

Tips:

  • Don't skip leg day!
  • Choose weights that challenge you, but allow you to complete the desired number of reps.
  • Rest for 1-2 minutes between sets.

Day 4 - Shoulders and Traps

On day 4, we will focus on building strength and muscle in the shoulders and traps. Here is a sample workout schedule:

Workout

FAQ

1. How many days a week should I workout?

It is recommended to workout at least 3-4 days a week for optimal results. However, if you have specific fitness goals, you may need to workout more or less frequently.

2. Can I change the exercises in the workout schedule?

Yes, you can customize the workout schedule to fit your personal fitness goals and preferences. Just make sure to target all major muscle groups.

3. How long should I rest between sets?

It is recommended to rest for 1-2 minutes between sets to allow your muscles to recover.
