What is Hi Tea?

Hi Tea, also known as Afternoon Tea, is a British tradition that originated in the 19th century. It is a light meal that is typically served in the late afternoon, between lunch and dinner. Hi Tea consists of a variety of finger foods, such as sandwiches, scones, pastries, and cakes, all served on a three-tiered stand. It is usually accompanied by tea, coffee, or other beverages.

Etiquette of Hi Tea

Hi Tea is a formal affair and has certain etiquette rules that should be followed. Firstly, dress appropriately for the occasion. Women should wear a dress or a skirt with a blouse, while men should wear a suit or a jacket and tie. Secondly, wait for the host or hostess to invite you to sit down before you do so. Thirdly, use the correct cutlery and crockery, and always place your napkin on your lap. Lastly, be polite and converse with your fellow guests in a respectful manner.

What to expect at a Hi Tea event

When you attend a Hi Tea event, you can expect to be served a variety of finger foods, as mentioned earlier. The tea will be served in a teapot, and there will be a selection of tea blends to choose from. The event will be hosted in a formal setting, such as a hotel or a restaurant, and there will be other guests in attendance. The atmosphere will be relaxed and convivial, with light conversation and laughter.

Tips for hosting your own Hi Tea party

If you want to host your own Hi Tea party, here are some tips to help you pull it off successfully:

  • Choose a theme for your Hi Tea party, such as a vintage or floral theme.
  • Send out invitations in advance to your guests, specifying the dress code and the time and date of the event.
  • Prepare a variety of finger foods, such as sandwiches, scones, and cakes. Make sure to include vegetarian and gluten-free options for your guests.
  • Set the table with a three-tiered stand, teapot, teacups and saucers, and other crockery and cutlery.
  • Choose a selection of tea blends for your guests to choose from.
  • Make sure to be a gracious host and engage your guests in conversation.

FAQ

What is the difference between Hi Tea and Afternoon Tea?

Hi Tea and Afternoon Tea are two terms that are often used interchangeably. However, some people believe that Hi Tea is more substantial and served later in the day, while Afternoon Tea is lighter and served earlier in the afternoon.

What is the origin of Hi Tea?

Hi Tea originated in the 19th century in England. It was traditionally served to the upper class as a light meal to tide them over until dinner.
