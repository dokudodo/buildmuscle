Recipe 1: Poha

Poha is a popular Indian breakfast recipe that is made from flattened rice flakes. It is a light and healthy breakfast that is perfect for people who are trying to lose weight. To make this recipe, you will need:

  • 1 cup poha (flattened rice flakes)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • Few curry leaves

How to Make Poha:

  1. Wash the poha in water and drain the excess water. Keep it aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and curry leaves. Let them splutter.
  3. Add onion and green chili and sauté until onion turns translucent.
  4. Add tomato, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook until tomato becomes soft.
  5. Add the washed poha and mix well. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot.

Pros: Poha is low in calories, high in fiber, and easy to digest. It is also a good source of carbohydrates and provides instant energy.

Cons: Poha can be high in sodium if you add too much salt. It is also important to use the right amount of oil to avoid excess calories.

Recipe 2: Oats Idli

Idli is a popular South Indian breakfast recipe that is usually made with rice and lentil batter. However, for people who are trying to lose weight, oats idli is a healthier option. To make this recipe, you will need:

  • 1 cup oats
  • 1/2 cup semolina
  • 1/2 cup curd
  • 1/4 cup grated carrot
  • 1/4 cup grated cabbage
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as required
  • Oil for greasing

How to Make Oats Idli:

  1. Grind the oats into a fine powder.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and curry leaves. Let them splutter.
  3. Add grated carrot and cabbage and sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Mix the oats powder, semolina, curd, sautéed vegetables, salt, and baking soda in a bowl.
  5. Add water as required to make a thick batter.
  6. Grease the idli molds with oil and pour the batter into them.
  7. Steam the idlis for 10-12 minutes or until they are cooked.
  8. Serve hot with chutney or sambar.

Pros: Oats are rich in fiber, protein, and antioxidants. They are also low in calories and help in weight loss. Oats idli is a healthier version of traditional idli and is perfect for people who are trying to lose weight.

Cons: Some people may find the taste of oats idli different from traditional idli. It is also important to use the right amount of baking soda to avoid any digestive issues.

Recipe 3: Vegetable Upma

Upma is a popular South Indian breakfast recipe that is usually made with semolina. However, to make it healthier and more nutritious, you can add vegetables to it. To make this recipe, you will need:

  • 1 cup semolina
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • Few curry leaves
  • 1/2 cup chopped vegetables (carrot, peas, beans, capsicum)

How to Make Vegetable Upma:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and curry leaves. Let them splutter.
  2. Add onion and green chili and sauté until onion turns translucent.
  3. Add tomato, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook until tomato becomes soft.
  4. Add the chopped vegetables and sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add water and bring it to a boil.
  6. Add semolina slowly and keep stirring to avoid lumps.
  7. Cover the pan and cook for 2-3 minutes or until the water is absorbed and the upma is cooked.
  8. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot.

Pros: Vegetable upma is a healthy and nutritious breakfast that is packed with vitamins and minerals. It is also low in calories and high in fiber, which makes it perfect for people who are trying to lose weight.

Cons: Some people may not like the taste of upma. It is also important to use the right amount of water and semolina to avoid any digestive issues.

Recipe 4: Moong Dal Cheela

Cheela is a popular North Indian breakfast recipe that is usually made with gram flour. However, for people who are trying to lose weight, moong dal cheela is a healthier option. To make this recipe, you will need:

  • 1 cup moong dal
  • 1/4 cup rice flour
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as required
  • Oil for cooking

How to Make Moong Dal Cheela:

  1. Wash and soak the moong dal in water for 3-4 hours.
  2. Grind the moong dal into a fine paste.
  3. Mix the moong dal paste, rice flour, chopped onion, tomato, green chili, cumin seeds, turmeric powder, and salt in a bowl.
  4. Add water as required to make a thin batter.
  5. Heat a non-stick pan and grease it with oil.
  6. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the pan and spread it in a circular motion.
  7. Cook the cheela on medium flame until it turns golden brown on both sides.
  8. Serve hot with chutney or aloo sabzi.
