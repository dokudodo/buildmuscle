Week 1

The first week of army basic training is all about general orientation and getting to know your fellow recruits. You will undergo medical and dental exams, receive your uniform and equipment, and attend briefings on army policies and procedures. You will also receive your first physical fitness assessment, which includes push-ups, sit-ups, and a 2-mile run.

Day 1

On the first day, you will report to the reception station, where you will undergo an initial health screening and receive your first haircut. You will then be transported to your basic training unit, where you will be introduced to your drill sergeant and receive your initial equipment issue.

Day 2-7

During the rest of the week, you will attend briefings on army policies and procedures, undergo medical and dental exams, and receive your first physical fitness assessment. You will also receive basic weapons training and attend classes on basic combat skills.

Week 2

The second week of army basic training is all about physical fitness and marksmanship. You will spend most of your time on the firing range, learning how to shoot accurately with a variety of weapons. You will also continue to participate in physical fitness training, including running, push-ups, and sit-ups.
