How To Check Apple Watch Workout Calories Burned

The Apple Watch tracks your workouts using its built-in sensors and algorithms. To check your calories burned during a workout:

  1. Open the Workout app on your Apple Watch
  2. Select the workout type you want to track
  3. Start your workout
  4. Swipe left on the workout summary screen to view your calories burned

Alternatively, you can check your calories burned on your iPhone by opening the Activity app and selecting the workout you want to view.

Step By Step Guide To Apple Watch Workout Calories Burned

The Apple Watch estimates your calories burned based on your heart rate, age, height, weight, and gender. Here is a step-by-step guide on how the Apple Watch calculates your calories burned:

  1. The Apple Watch measures your heart rate using its optical heart sensor
  2. It uses your heart rate data to estimate your oxygen consumption and metabolic rate
  3. It combines your metabolic rate with your age, height, weight, and gender to calculate your calories burned

It is important to note that the accuracy of the Apple Watch's calorie tracking may vary based on individual factors such as fitness level and body composition.

Tips To Increase Apple Watch Workout Calories Burned

If you want to burn more calories during your workouts, here are some tips:

  • Increase the intensity of your workouts by adding resistance or increasing your speed
  • Include high-intensity interval training (HIIT) in your workouts
  • Try new types of workouts to challenge your body and burn more calories
  • Stay consistent with your workouts to build endurance and increase your calorie burn over time

Solution To Apple Watch Workout Calories Burned Not Accurate

If you find that your Apple Watch is not accurately tracking your calories burned during workouts, try these solutions:

  • Make sure your Apple Watch is snug on your wrist and the heart rate sensor is in contact with your skin
  • Update your personal information such as height, weight, and age in the Health app on your iPhone
  • Calibrate your Apple Watch for better accuracy by going for a 20-minute outdoor walk or run with your iPhone

FAQs About Apple Watch Workout Calories Burned

Q: Can I track my calories burned during non-workout activities?

A: Yes, the Apple Watch tracks your active and passive calories burned throughout the day, including during non-workout activities such as walking and standing.

Q: Does the Apple Watch track calories burned for all types of workouts?

A: Yes, the Apple Watch can track calories burned for a variety of workout types including running, swimming, cycling, and strength training.

Q: Is the Apple Watch's calorie tracking accurate?

A: The accuracy of the Apple Watch's calorie tracking may vary based on individual factors such as fitness level and body composition. However, it is generally considered to be a reliable estimate.

Pros and Cons of Using Apple Watch To Track Calories Burned

Pros:

  • Convenient way to track your workouts and calories burned
  • Provides motivation to stay active and reach fitness goals
  • Tracks both active and passive calories burned throughout the day

Cons:

  • May not be 100% accurate for everyone
  • Requires an Apple Watch and iPhone
  • May drain the battery on your Apple Watch and iPhone faster
