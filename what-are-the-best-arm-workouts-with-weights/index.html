Are you looking to build bigger, stronger, and more defined arms? If so, then you need to incorporate the best arm workouts with weights into your fitness routine. These exercises are designed to target your biceps, triceps, and forearms, helping you to achieve your muscle-building goals. In this article, we will explore some of the most effective arm workouts with weights that you can try out today.

Bicep Curls

Bicep curls are one of the best arm workouts with weights that you can do. This exercise targets your biceps, which are the muscles that are responsible for flexing your arm at the elbow. To do bicep curls, follow these steps:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a dumbbell in each hand, with your palms facing forward.
  2. Curl the weights up towards your shoulders, keeping your elbows close to your sides.
  3. Pause at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the weights back down to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for 10-12 repetitions.

Bicep curls can be done with a variety of equipment, including dumbbells, barbells, or resistance bands. You can also vary the grip on the weights to target different areas of your biceps.

Hammer Curls

Hammer curls are a variation of bicep curls that target your brachioradialis muscle, which is located on the outside of your forearm. To do hammer curls, follow these steps:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a dumbbell in each hand, with your palms facing inwards.
  2. Curl the weights up towards your shoulders, keeping your elbows close to your sides.
  3. Pause at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the weights back down to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for 10-12 repetitions.

Hammer curls can also be done with a variety of equipment, including dumbbells, barbells, or resistance bands. Varying the weight and grip can also help target different areas of your forearm.

Tricep Extensions

Tricep extensions are one of the best arm workouts with weights for targeting your triceps, which are the muscles that are responsible for extending your arm at the elbow. To do tricep extensions, follow these steps:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a dumbbell in both hands, with your arms raised above your head.
  2. Bend your elbows, lowering the weight behind your head.
  3. Pause at the bottom of the movement, then extend your arms back up to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for 10-12 repetitions.

Tricep extensions can be done with a variety of equipment, including dumbbells, barbells, or resistance bands. You can also vary the grip on the weights to target different areas of your triceps.

Dips

Dips are a bodyweight exercise that target your triceps, chest, and shoulders. To do dips, follow these steps:

  1. Find a stable surface, such as parallel bars or the edge of a bench.
  2. Place your hands shoulder-width apart on the surface and lift your feet off the ground.
  3. Lower your body down towards the ground, bending your elbows.
  4. Pause at the bottom of the movement, then push back up to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for 10-12 repetitions.

Dips can be made more challenging by adding weight or by leaning forward to target your chest more.

Push-Ups

Push-ups are another bodyweight exercise that target your chest, triceps, and shoulders. To do push-ups, follow these steps:

  1. Start in a plank position, with your hands shoulder-width apart.
  2. Lower your body down towards the ground, keeping your elbows close to your sides.
  3. Pause at the bottom of the movement, then push back up to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for 10-12 repetitions.

Push-ups can be made more challenging by adding weight, elevating your feet, or by doing different variations such as diamond push-ups or one-arm push-ups.

Chin-Ups

Chin-ups are a bodyweight exercise that target your biceps, back, and shoulders. To do chin-ups, follow these steps:

  1. Find a horizontal bar that is high enough for you to hang from.
  2. Grab the bar with an underhand grip, with your hands shoulder-width apart.
  3. Pull your body up towards the bar, keeping your elbows close to your sides.
  4. Pause at the top of the movement, then slowly lower your body back down to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for 10-12 repetitions.

Chin-ups can be made more challenging by adding weight or by doing different variations such as wide-grip pull-ups or negative chin-ups.

Reverse Curls

Reverse curls are a variation of bicep curls that target your brachialis muscle, which is located on the inside of your upper arm. To do reverse curls, follow these steps:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a dumbbell in each hand, with your palms facing downwards.
  2. Curl the weights up towards your shoulders, keeping your elbows close to your sides.
  3. Pause at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the weights back down to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for 10-12 repetitions.

Reverse curls can also be done with a variety of equipment, including dumbbells, barbells, or resistance bands. Varying the weight and grip can also help target different areas of your brachialis muscle.

Wrist Curls

Wrist curls are an arm workout with weights that target your forearm muscles. To do wrist curls, follow these steps:

  1. Sit on a bench with your forearms resting on your thighs and a dumbbell in each hand.
  2. Curl the weights up towards your wrists, keeping your forearms stationary.
  3. Pause at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the weights back down to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for 10-12 repetitions.

Wrist curls can be done with a variety of equipment, including dumbbells, barbells, or resistance bands. You can also vary the weight and grip to target different areas of your forearms.

Concentration Curls

Concentration curls are a variation of bicep curls that target your biceps and forearms. To do concentration curls, follow these steps:

  1. Sit on a bench with your feet flat on the ground and a dumbbell in one hand.
  2. Rest your elbow on the inside of your thigh and curl the weight up towards your shoulder.
  3. Pause at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the weight back down to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for 10-12 repetitions, then switch to the other arm.

Concentration curls can be done with a variety of equipment, including dumbbells, barbells, or resistance bands. They are a great exercise for building both biceps and forearms.
