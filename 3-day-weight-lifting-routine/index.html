In this article, we will be discussing a 3 day weight lifting routine that is perfect for beginners looking to build muscle. We will cover everything from how to get started, step by step instructions, tips, solutions, FAQ, and the pros and cons of this routine. This routine is designed to be done in three days per week, making it great for those who are just starting out or those who have a busy schedule. So, let's get started!

Getting Started

Before you begin any weight lifting routine, it is important to consult with your doctor to make sure that you are healthy enough to participate. Once you have received the green light, it's time to get started!

The 3 day weight lifting routine that we will be discussing consists of three days of weight lifting per week. This is a great routine for beginners because it allows for plenty of rest and recovery time between workouts.

Step by Step Instructions

Day 1: Chest and Triceps

  • Bench Press: 3 sets of 10 reps
  • Incline Bench Press: 3 sets of 10 reps
  • Dumbbell Flys: 3 sets of 10 reps
  • Tricep Pushdowns: 3 sets of 10 reps
  • Skull Crushers: 3 sets of 10 reps

Day 2: Back and Biceps

  • Deadlifts: 3 sets of 10 reps
  • Lat Pulldowns: 3 sets of 10 reps
  • Barbell Rows: 3 sets of 10 reps
  • Hammer Curls: 3 sets of 10 reps
  • Preacher Curls: 3 sets of 10 reps

Day 3: Legs and Shoulders

  • Squats: 3 sets of 10 reps
  • Lunges: 3 sets of 10 reps
  • Leg Press: 3 sets of 10 reps
  • Shoulder Press: 3 sets of 10 reps
  • Side Lateral Raises: 3 sets of 10 reps

Tips for Success

Here are some tips to help you succeed with the 3 day weight lifting routine:

  • Start with light weights and gradually increase the weight as you get stronger.
  • Make sure to warm up before each workout to prevent injury.
  • Stay hydrated and eat a healthy diet to fuel your workouts.
  • Get plenty of rest and recovery time between workouts.

Solutions to Common Problems

If you experience any problems while doing the 3 day weight lifting routine, here are some solutions:

  • If you experience pain or discomfort, stop the exercise immediately and consult with your doctor.
  • If you are not seeing results, make sure that you are using proper form and technique and that you are lifting enough weight.
  • If you are feeling tired or fatigued, make sure that you are getting enough rest and recovery time between workouts.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about the 3 day weight lifting routine:

  • Q: How long should each workout be?
  • A: Each workout should be around 45-60 minutes.
  • Q: How often should I do this routine?
  • A: This routine should be done three days per week.
  • Q: Can I do cardio on the same days as weight lifting?
  • A: Yes, but make sure to do cardio after weight lifting to prevent injury.

Pros and Cons

Here are some pros and cons of the 3 day weight lifting routine:

Pros:

  • Great for beginners
  • Allows for plenty of rest and recovery time
  • Targets all major muscle groups

Cons:

  • May not be enough for advanced lifters
  • May not be ideal for those with busy schedules

Overall, the 3 day weight lifting routine is a great option for beginners looking to build muscle. It targets all major muscle groups and allows for plenty of rest and recovery time between workouts. Just remember to consult with your doctor before starting any weight lifting routine and to use proper form and technique to prevent injury.
