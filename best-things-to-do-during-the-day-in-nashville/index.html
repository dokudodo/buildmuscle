Welcome to Nashville, the country music capital of the world! There's so much to see and do here during the day, you might not know where to start. In this article, we'll cover the best things to do in Nashville during the day, from visiting iconic music venues to exploring beautiful parks. Let's dive in!

Table of Contents

  • Visit Iconic Music Venues
  • Explore Beautiful Parks
  • Learn at Museums
  • Shop in Nashville
  • Try Delicious Food

Visit Iconic Music Venues

Nashville is known for its music scene, so it's no surprise that visiting music venues is one of the best things to do during the day. The Ryman Auditorium is a must-visit, as it's a National Historic Landmark and has hosted countless legendary musicians. The Grand Ole Opry is another iconic venue that you shouldn't miss, as it's been broadcasting live country music since 1925. If you're looking for something more intimate, check out The Bluebird Cafe, where you can see up-and-coming songwriters perform in an intimate setting.

Explore Beautiful Parks

Nashville is home to several beautiful parks that are perfect for daytime exploration. Centennial Park is a popular spot, as it features a replica of the Parthenon and plenty of green space. If you're looking for something more natural, head to Percy Warner Park, where you can hike or bike on miles of trails. Another great option is Radnor Lake State Park, which offers stunning views of the lake and surrounding forest.

Learn at Museums

If you're interested in history or art, Nashville has several great museums to explore during the day. The Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum is a must-visit for any music lover, as it features exhibits on country music's rich history. The Frist Art Museum is another great option, as it showcases rotating exhibits of contemporary art. Finally, the Tennessee State Museum is a great place to learn about the state's history, from Native American artifacts to Civil War memorabilia.

Shop in Nashville

Nashville is home to several great shopping districts that are perfect for daytime exploration. The Gulch is a trendy neighborhood that features boutique shops and trendy restaurants. If you're looking for something more traditional, head to Hillsboro Village, where you can find everything from vintage clothing to handmade crafts. Finally, the Nashville Farmers' Market is a great place to shop for locally grown produce, artisanal cheeses, and handmade crafts.

Try Delicious Food

No trip to Nashville is complete without trying some of the city's delicious food. Hot chicken is a local specialty, and you can find it at several restaurants around town. Hattie B's is a popular spot, as they serve up hot chicken in a variety of heat levels. If you're looking for something more upscale, head to Husk, where you can try Southern-inspired dishes made with locally sourced ingredients. Finally, don't forget to check out the food trucks around town, which serve up everything from tacos to cupcakes.

Final Thoughts

There's so much to see and do in Nashville during the day, from visiting iconic music venues to exploring beautiful parks. Whatever your interests, you're sure to find something that you'll love in this vibrant city. So what are you waiting for? Start planning your trip to Nashville today!
