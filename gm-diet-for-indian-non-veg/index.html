If you are an Indian non-vegetarian looking to lose weight, then you might have come across the GM diet. This diet plan is gaining popularity among weight watchers due to its quick results. But, as an Indian non-veg, you might be wondering if this diet is even feasible for you. In this article, we will guide you through the GM diet for Indian non-veg and give you tips to make it work for you.

What is GM Diet?

GM diet or General Motors diet is a seven-day diet plan that claims to help you lose weight up to 17 pounds or 7.7 kgs. The diet plan consists of eating mostly fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins.

How does GM Diet work?

The GM diet works by restricting your calorie intake to 1200-1400 calories per day for seven days. By doing so, you create a calorie deficit that forces your body to burn fat for energy.

How to do GM Diet for Indian Non Veg?

Here is a step-by-step guide to do the GM diet for Indian non-veg:

Day 1:

On day one, you are allowed to eat only fruits except for bananas. You can eat any fruit of your choice and drink plenty of water. You can have up to 8-10 servings of fruits on this day.

Day 2:

On day two, you are allowed to eat only vegetables. You can eat them raw or cooked, and you can add a potato for breakfast. You can have up to 8-10 servings of vegetables on this day.

Day 3:

On day three, you are allowed to eat both fruits and vegetables except for bananas and potatoes. You can have up to 8-10 servings of fruits and vegetables on this day.

Day 4:

On day four, you are allowed to eat only bananas and milk. You can have up to 8 bananas and 3 glasses of milk on this day.

Day 5:

On day five, you can eat lean protein like chicken, fish, or beef. You can have up to 6 tomatoes and drink plenty of water.

Day 6:

On day six, you can have lean protein like chicken, fish, or beef along with vegetables. You can have up to 8-10 servings of vegetables and 2 servings of protein on this day.

Day 7:

On day seven, you can have brown rice, vegetables, and fruit juice. You can have up to 8-10 servings of vegetables, 2 servings of brown rice, and fruit juice.

Tips to Make GM Diet Work for Indian Non Veg

Here are some tips to make the GM diet work for Indian non-veg:

  • Replace beef with chicken or fish to make it more feasible for Indian non-veg.
  • Use Indian spices like turmeric, cumin, coriander, and chili powder to add flavor to your meals.
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated throughout the day.
  • Avoid processed foods and sugar during the seven days.
  • Exercise for at least 30 minutes every day to maximize weight loss.

Pros and Cons of GM Diet for Indian Non Veg

Here are some pros and cons of GM diet for Indian non-veg:

Pros:

  • Quick weight loss results
  • Easy to follow
  • No need for expensive supplements or equipment

Cons:

  • Not a sustainable long-term solution for weight loss
  • Limited food choices
  • May lead to nutrient deficiencies

FAQ

Can I do GM diet if I am a non-vegetarian?

Yes, you can do the GM diet if you are a non-vegetarian. You can replace beef with chicken or fish and follow the same guidelines as a vegetarian.

Can I exercise during the GM diet?

Yes, you can exercise during the GM diet. We recommend doing low to moderate intensity exercises like walking, jogging, or yoga.

Can I drink alcohol during the GM diet?

No, you should avoid alcohol during the GM diet as it can hinder your weight loss progress.

Conclusion

The GM diet can be a great way to kickstart your weight loss journey as an Indian non-veg. By following the guidelines and tips mentioned in this article, you can make the GM diet work for you. However, we recommend consulting with your doctor before starting any diet plan.
