4 Day Split Vs Full Body Which One Is Better

If you're looking to start a new workout routine, you may be wondering whether a 4 day split or full body workout is better for you. Both have their advantages and disadvantages, and it ultimately comes down to your fitness goals and personal preferences. In this article, we'll break down the differences between the two and help you make an informed decision.

Description

A 4 day split is a workout routine that involves working out different muscle groups on different days. For example, you might work on your chest and triceps on Monday, back and biceps on Tuesday, rest on Wednesday, legs on Thursday, and shoulders and abs on Friday. This allows you to focus on specific muscle groups and give them more attention.

A full body workout, on the other hand, involves working out all major muscle groups in one session. This means you'll do exercises for your chest, back, legs, shoulders, biceps, and triceps all in one workout. This type of workout is great for beginners and those with limited time, as it allows you to get a full body workout in a shorter amount of time.

How To

When deciding between a 4 day split and full body workout, consider your fitness goals and schedule. If you're looking to build muscle and have more time to devote to your workouts, a 4 day split may be the better option. If you're a beginner or have limited time, a full body workout can be effective.

4 Day Split

If you decide to go with a 4 day split, you'll want to choose exercises that target different muscle groups on each day. Here's an example:

  • Monday: Chest and triceps
  • Tuesday: Back and biceps
  • Wednesday: Rest
  • Thursday: Legs
  • Friday: Shoulders and abs

For each day, choose 3-4 exercises that target the muscle groups you're working on. For example, on Monday you might do bench presses, tricep dips, and cable flyes. Aim for 3-4 sets of 8-12 reps for each exercise.

Full Body Workout

If you're doing a full body workout, choose exercises that target all major muscle groups. Here's an example:

  • Bench press
  • Deadlifts
  • Squats
  • Chin-ups
  • Shoulder press

For each exercise, aim for 3-4 sets of 8-12 reps. Rest for 1-2 minutes between sets.

Step by Step

Here are the steps you should follow when deciding between a 4 day split and full body workout:

  1. Determine your fitness goals
  2. Consider your schedule and how much time you have to devote to working out
  3. Choose a workout routine that fits your goals and schedule
  4. Select exercises that target the muscle groups you want to work on
  5. Aim for 3-4 sets of 8-12 reps for each exercise
  6. Rest for 1-2 minutes between sets

Tips

  • Be consistent with your workout routine
  • Focus on proper form and technique
  • Gradually increase the weight you're lifting over time
  • Include rest days in your routine to allow your muscles to recover
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes protein to support muscle growth

Solution

Ultimately, the decision between a 4 day split and full body workout comes down to your fitness goals and personal preferences. If you're looking to build muscle and have more time to devote to your workouts, a 4 day split may be the better option. If you're a beginner or have limited time, a full body workout can be effective.

FAQ

Which is better, a 4 day split or full body workout?

It depends on your fitness goals and schedule. If you're looking to build muscle and have more time to devote to your workouts, a 4 day split may be the better option. If you're a beginner or have limited time, a full body workout can be effective.

How many times a week should I work out?

It's generally recommended to work out at least 3-4 times a week for optimal results. However, the frequency and intensity of your workouts will depend on your fitness goals and schedule.

Pros and Cons

4 Day Split Pros:

  • Allows you to focus on specific muscle groups
  • Can help you build muscle faster
  • Allows for more variety in your workouts

4 Day Split Cons:

  • Requires more time and dedication
  • Can be more tiring and lead to burnout
  • May not be ideal for beginners or those with limited time

Full Body Workout Pros:

  • Can be effective for beginners or those with limited time
  • All major muscle groups are targeted in one workout
  • Can help you build endurance and stamina

Full Body Workout Cons:

  • May not be as effective for building muscle as a 4 day split
  • Can be more challenging to perform all exercises in one workout
  • May not provide enough variety in your workouts

Ultimately, the decision between a 4 day split and full body workout comes down to your fitness goals and personal preferences. Consider your schedule and how much time you have to devote to working out, and choose a routine that fits your needs. Be consistent with your workouts and focus on proper form and technique to see the best results.
