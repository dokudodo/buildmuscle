How Often Does Cardiac Ablation Work? | FAQs, Tips & More

Cardiac ablation is a medical procedure that uses catheters to destroy or scar tissue in the heart that causes abnormal heart rhythms. This article will discuss the success rate of cardiac ablation, how it works, and answer frequently asked questions about the procedure.

How Cardiac Ablation Works

The procedure involves inserting thin, flexible catheters through a vein in the groin and threading them up to the heart. Once in place, the catheter delivers energy to destroy a small area of heart tissue responsible for the abnormal heart rhythm. The procedure can take several hours to complete.

The goal of cardiac ablation is to restore a normal heart rhythm and reduce symptoms such as palpitations, shortness of breath, and fainting. The procedure is typically performed under local anesthesia and patients may need to stay in the hospital overnight for observation.

Success Rate of Cardiac Ablation

The success rate of cardiac ablation varies depending on the type of arrhythmia being treated and the skill of the cardiologist performing the procedure. Overall, the success rate of cardiac ablation is around 70-90%. However, some patients may require a second or even third procedure if the arrhythmia recurs.

Factors that can affect the success rate of cardiac ablation include the size and location of the area being ablated, the type of catheter used, and the experience of the cardiologist performing the procedure.

FAQs about Cardiac Ablation

Who is a candidate for cardiac ablation?

Patients with abnormal heart rhythms that do not respond to medications or other treatments may be candidates for cardiac ablation. Your cardiologist will perform a comprehensive evaluation to determine if cardiac ablation is appropriate for you.

Is cardiac ablation a safe procedure?

Like all medical procedures, there are risks associated with cardiac ablation. These risks include bleeding, infection, damage to blood vessels or heart tissue, and abnormal heart rhythm. However, the risk of serious complications is low and most patients experience no major problems.

What is recovery like after cardiac ablation?

Patients may experience some discomfort at the catheter insertion site and may need to limit physical activity for several days after the procedure. Your cardiologist will provide specific instructions for recovery and follow-up care.

Tips for Before and After Cardiac Ablation

Before the procedure, your doctor may recommend that you stop taking certain medications or change your diet to prepare for the procedure. After the procedure, it is important to follow all instructions for recovery and take any medications as prescribed.

Other tips for before and after cardiac ablation include:

  • Arrange for someone to drive you home after the procedure
  • Wear comfortable clothing to the hospital
  • Follow a heart-healthy diet and exercise program
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption
  • Attend all follow-up appointments with your cardiologist

Pros and Cons of Cardiac Ablation

Pros:

  • Can be an effective treatment for abnormal heart rhythms
  • May reduce the need for medications or other interventions
  • Low risk of serious complications

Cons:

  • Not always successful in restoring a normal heart rhythm
  • May require multiple procedures
  • Can be expensive and may not be covered by insurance

Overall, cardiac ablation can be an effective treatment option for patients with abnormal heart rhythms. However, it is important to discuss the risks and benefits of the procedure with your cardiologist to determine if it is the right choice for you.
