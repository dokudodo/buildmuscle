Easy Full Body Workout With Weights

If you're looking for a quick and easy full body workout with weights, look no further. This workout is perfect for beginners and those who don't have a lot of time to spend in the gym. We'll cover everything you need to know, including how to do the exercises, step by step instructions, tips, solutions, FAQs, and the pros and cons of this workout. So, let's get started!

  • How to
  • Step by Step

How to:

To do this full body workout with weights, you'll need a set of dumbbells or kettlebells. If you don't have any, you can use water bottles or cans of food as a substitute. The weight should be challenging but not too heavy that you can't complete the exercises with proper form. Start with a weight that feels comfortable and increase it as you get stronger.

Exercises:

The following exercises are part of this full body workout:

  • Squats
  • Lunges
  • Push-ups
  • Bent-over rows
  • Shoulder press
  • Bicep curls
  • Tricep extensions
  • Plank

Step by Step:

Perform each exercise for 3 sets of 12-15 reps. Rest for 30 seconds between sets and exercises. Increase the weight as you get stronger. Here are the step by step instructions for each exercise:

Squats:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold the weights at your sides.
  2. Lower your body as if you're sitting in a chair until your thighs are parallel to the ground.
  3. Push through your heels to return to starting position.

Lunges:

  1. Stand with your feet hip-width apart and hold the weights at your sides.
  2. Step forward with one foot and lower your body until your front thigh is parallel to the ground.
  3. Push through your front heel to return to starting position.
  4. Repeat with the other leg.

Push-ups:

  1. Start in a plank position with your hands shoulder-width apart and your feet together.
  2. Lower your body until your chest touches the ground.
  3. Push through your hands to return to starting position.

Bent-over rows:

  1. Stand with your feet hip-width apart and hold the weights in front of your thighs.
  2. Bend forward at the waist and keep your back straight.
  3. Pull the weights up to your chest, squeezing your shoulder blades together.
  4. Lower the weights back to starting position.

Shoulder press:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold the weights at shoulder height, palms facing forward.
  2. Push the weights up overhead until your arms are straight.
  3. Lower the weights back to starting position.

Bicep curls:

  1. Stand with your feet hip-width apart and hold the weights at your sides, palms facing forward.
  2. Curl the weights up to your shoulders, squeezing your biceps.
  3. Lower the weights back to starting position.

Tricep extensions:

  1. Stand with your feet hip-width apart and hold one weight with both hands behind your head.
  2. Extend your arms up until the weight is overhead.
  3. Lower the weight back to starting position behind your head.

Plank:

  1. Start in a push-up position with your hands shoulder-width apart and your feet together.
  2. Lower yourself onto your forearms and hold your body in a straight line from head to heels.
  3. Hold for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Tips:

Here are some tips to get the most out of this full body workout:

  • Start with a weight that feels comfortable and increase it as you get stronger.
  • Focus on proper form for each exercise to avoid injury.
  • Breathe deeply and exhale on the exertion (the hardest part of the exercise).
  • Rest for 30 seconds between sets and exercises.
  • Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout.

Solution:

This easy full body workout with weights is a great solution for those who want to get a full body workout in a short amount of time. It targets all of your major muscle groups and can be done at home or in the gym. Plus, it's easy to modify the exercises to make them more challenging as you get stronger.

FAQ:

Here are some frequently asked questions about this full body workout:

  • How often should I do this workout? Aim to do this workout 2-3 times per week, with at least one day of rest in between.
  • Can I do this workout without weights? Yes, you can modify the exercises to use just your body weight.
  • How long will it take to see results? Results will vary depending on your fitness level and diet, but you should start to see and feel changes in your body within a few weeks.

Pros and Cons:

Here are the pros and cons of this easy full body workout with weights:

Pros:

  • Targets all major muscle groups
  • Can be done at home or in the gym
  • Easy to modify as you get stronger
  • Quick and efficient

Cons:

  • May not be challenging enough for advanced lifters
  • Requires equipment (dumbbells or kettlebells)
  • May not be suitable for those with injuries or medical conditions

Overall, this easy full body workout with weights is a great option for beginners or those who want to get a quick and efficient full body workout. With proper form and consistency, you'll start to see and feel changes in your body in no time.
