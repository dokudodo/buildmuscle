Are you one of those early risers who hit the gym without having breakfast? Or do you prefer to run on an empty stomach in the morning? Whatever your preference is, it's crucial to know what happens if you do cardio on an empty stomach. In this article, we'll explore the science behind it, its benefits, and drawbacks, and whether it's a good idea for you.

What Happens If You Do Cardio on an Empty Stomach?

When you do cardio on an empty stomach, your body burns fat as fuel instead of carbohydrates. This phenomenon is known as "fasted cardio." During fasting, your body has low glycogen stores, making it more likely to use fat for energy. As a result, you burn more calories, lose weight, and improve your body composition.

However, fasted cardio also has its drawbacks. When you exercise without food, your body relies on stored energy, which can lead to fatigue, dizziness, and lightheadedness. Moreover, your body may break down muscle tissue for energy, which can hamper your muscle growth goals.

How to Do Cardio on an Empty Stomach?

If you're planning to do cardio on an empty stomach, there are a few things you need to keep in mind. Firstly, start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workout. Secondly, stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water before, during, and after your workout. Thirdly, listen to your body and stop exercising if you feel dizzy or lightheaded.

Additionally, if you're doing high-intensity workouts like HIIT, it's better to eat a small meal or snack before exercising. HIIT requires more energy, and doing it on an empty stomach can lead to muscle breakdown and fatigue.

Step by Step Guide to Doing Cardio on an Empty Stomach

Here's a step-by-step guide to doing cardio on an empty stomach:

  1. Wake up early in the morning and drink a glass of water to hydrate yourself.
  2. Wear comfortable workout clothes and shoes.
  3. Do a 5-10 minute warm-up to prepare your body for exercise.
  4. Start with low-intensity exercises like walking, jogging, or cycling for 20-30 minutes.
  5. Gradually increase the duration and intensity of your workout as you get fitter.
  6. Stay hydrated by drinking water during and after your workout.
  7. Cool down for 5-10 minutes by doing stretching exercises.
  8. Eat a healthy breakfast after your workout to replenish your energy stores.

Tips for Doing Cardio on an Empty Stomach

Here are some tips to make your fasted cardio workout more effective:

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workout.
  • Use a heart rate monitor to track your heart rate during exercise.
  • Choose low-impact exercises like walking or cycling to reduce the risk of injury.
  • Eat a healthy breakfast after your workout to replenish your energy stores.

Pros and Cons of Doing Cardio on an Empty Stomach

Like any other workout, fasted cardio has its pros and cons. Here are some of them:

Pros

  • Burns more fat and calories.
  • Improves body composition.
  • Boosts metabolism.
  • Can be an effective weight loss strategy.

Cons

  • Can lead to fatigue, dizziness, and lightheadedness.
  • Can hamper muscle growth goals.
  • May increase the risk of injury.
  • Not suitable for high-intensity workouts like HIIT.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about doing cardio on an empty stomach:

Is fasted cardio better for weight loss?

Yes, fasted cardio can be an effective weight loss strategy as it burns more fat and calories.

Can I do high-intensity workouts like HIIT on an empty stomach?

It's not recommended to do high-intensity workouts like HIIT on an empty stomach as it can lead to muscle breakdown and fatigue.

Should I eat after doing cardio on an empty stomach?

Yes, it's essential to eat a healthy breakfast after doing cardio on an empty stomach to replenish your energy stores.

Conclusion

Doing cardio on an empty stomach has its benefits, but it's not suitable for everyone. If you're new to exercise or have health issues, it's better to eat a small meal or snack before exercising. Moreover, if you're doing high-intensity workouts, it's better to eat something before exercising. Remember to stay hydrated, listen to your body, and stop exercising if you feel dizzy or lightheaded.
