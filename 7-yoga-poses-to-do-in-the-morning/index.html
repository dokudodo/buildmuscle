Starting your day with yoga can be a great way to energize your body and mind. Incorporating yoga into your morning routine can help you start your day on a positive and calm note. Here are 7 yoga poses to do in the morning:

Table of Contents

  • Mountain Pose
  • Tree Pose
  • Downward Facing Dog Pose
  • Cobra Pose
  • Seated Forward Bend Pose
  • Child Pose

Mountain Pose

Mountain Pose is a great way to start your yoga practice in the morning. It helps you focus on your breath and grounds you for the day ahead.

How to:

  1. Stand at the front of your mat with your feet together.
  2. Spread your toes wide and press them into the mat.
  3. Engage your thigh muscles and lift your kneecaps.
  4. Lengthen your spine and roll your shoulders back and down.
  5. Close your eyes and take a few deep breaths.

Tips:

  • Try to distribute your weight evenly through your feet.
  • Keep your chin parallel to the ground.

Pros:

  • Improves posture
  • Reduces stress and anxiety
  • Increases focus and concentration

Cons:

  • May not be suitable for those with knee or ankle injuries

Tree Pose

Tree Pose is a great way to improve balance and concentration. It also helps stretch the hips and thighs.

How to:

  1. Stand at the front of your mat with your feet together.
  2. Shift your weight onto your left foot.
  3. Bend your right knee and place the sole of your right foot on your left thigh.
  4. Press your foot into your thigh and bring your hands to your heart.
  5. Hold for a few breaths and then switch sides.

Tips:

  • Focus your gaze on one point to help with balance.
  • Engage your core to help with balance.

Pros:

  • Improves balance and concentration
  • Strengthens legs
  • Opens hips

Cons:

  • May not be suitable for those with knee or ankle injuries

Downward Facing Dog Pose

Downward Facing Dog Pose is a great way to stretch the hamstrings, calves, and spine. It also helps calm the mind and relieve stress.

How to:

  1. Start on your hands and knees with your wrists under your shoulders and your knees under your hips.
  2. Lift your hips up and back, straightening your arms and legs.
  3. Press your hands and feet into the mat and lengthen your spine.
  4. Hold for a few breaths and then release.

Tips:

  • Keep your head and neck relaxed.
  • Bend your knees if you need to.

Pros:

  • Stretches hamstrings, calves, and spine
  • Relieves stress and anxiety
  • Strengthens arms and shoulders

Cons:

  • May not be suitable for those with wrist or shoulder injuries

Cobra Pose

Cobra Pose is a great way to stretch the chest, shoulders, and abdomen. It also helps improve posture and relieve stress.

How to:

  1. Lie on your stomach with your hands under your shoulders.
  2. Press your hands into the mat and lift your chest off the ground.
  3. Keep your elbows close to your sides and lengthen your spine.
  4. Hold for a few breaths and then release.

Tips:

  • Keep your shoulders relaxed.
  • Engage your core to help support your back.

Pros:

  • Stretches chest, shoulders, and abdomen
  • Improves posture
  • Relieves stress and fatigue

Cons:

  • May not be suitable for those with back injuries

Seated Forward Bend Pose

Seated Forward Bend Pose is a great way to stretch the hamstrings and lower back. It also helps calm the mind and relieve stress.

How to:

  1. Sit on the mat with your legs extended in front of you.
  2. Lengthen your spine and reach your arms up overhead.
  3. Slowly fold forward from your hips and reach for your feet.
  4. Hold for a few breaths and then release.

Tips:

  • Bend your knees if you need to.
  • Use a strap or towel to help you reach your feet.

Pros:

  • Stretches hamstrings and lower back
  • Calms the mind and relieves stress
  • Helps improve digestion

Cons:

  • May not be suitable for those with back or hamstring injuries

Child Pose

Child Pose is a great way to stretch the hips, thighs, and ankles. It also helps calm the mind and relieve stress.

How to:

  1. Kneel on the mat with your big toes touching and your knees hip-width apart.
  2. Lower your hips back toward your heels and stretch your arms out in front of you.
  3. Rest your forehead on the mat and breathe deeply.
  4. Hold for a few breaths and then release.

Tips:

  • Place a cushion or folded blanket under your hips for support.
  • Use a block or pillow under your forehead if it doesn't reach the ground.

Pros:

  • Stretches hips, thighs, and ankles
  • Calms the mind and relieves stress
  • Helps improve digestion

Cons:

  • May not be suitable for those with knee or ankle injuries

Corpse Pose

Corpse Pose is a great way to relax your body and mind. It also helps reduce stress and anxiety.

How to:

  1. Lie on your back with your arms and legs extended.
  2. Close your eyes and focus on your breath.
  3. Scan your body for any tension and release it.
  4. Stay in this pose for a few minutes.

Tips:

  • Use a blanket or pillow under your head and knees for support.
  • Try to stay still and relaxed.

Pros:

  • Relaxes body and mind
  • Reduces stress and anxiety
  • Helps improve sleep
