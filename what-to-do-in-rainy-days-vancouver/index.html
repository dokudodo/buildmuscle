What To Do In Rainy Days Vancouver Explore The City In A Different Way

Are you visiting Vancouver and encountering a rainy day? No need to worry! Vancouver is known for its rainy weather, and there are plenty of activities to enjoy even when it’s wet. In this article, we’ll share with you some of the best things to do in rainy days Vancouver.

Table of Contents:

  • Outdoor Activities
  • Indoor Activities
  • Food and Drinks
  • Shopping
  • Museum and Gallery

Outdoor Activities

Don’t let the rain discourage you from enjoying the outdoors. Vancouver is surrounded by mountains, forests, and the ocean, making it a great destination for outdoor activities, even in the rain.

1. Rainforest Walks

Put on your rain jacket and explore the lush rainforests of the Pacific Northwest. Stanley Park, Lynn Canyon Park, and Capilano Suspension Bridge Park are just a few of the places where you can take a guided tour or wander on your own.

2. Whale Watching

Don’t let the rain stop you from seeing the majestic whales that call the Pacific Northwest home. Hop on a boat tour and enjoy the scenery while staying dry under the boat’s covered deck.

3. Hot Springs

Relax in the natural hot springs while enjoying the rain. Harrison Hot Springs and the Skookumchuck Hot Springs are just a few of the places where you can soak in the warm water while enjoying the cool raindrops.

Indoor Activities

When the rain is pouring outside, there are plenty of indoor activities to keep you entertained in Vancouver.

1. Aquarium

The Vancouver Aquarium is a great place to spend a rainy day. With over 50,000 animals and exhibits featuring marine life from Canada’s Pacific Northwest, you’ll be entertained for hours.

2. Science World

Science World is an interactive science museum that is fun for all ages. With over 400 interactive exhibits, you can easily spend a whole day exploring and learning.

3. Movie Theatres

There’s nothing like watching a good movie on a rainy day. Vancouver has many movie theatres to choose from, including the Cineplex Odeon, Scotiabank Theatre, and Rio Theatre.

Food and Drinks

When it’s raining outside, it’s always a good time to indulge in some delicious food and drinks.

1. Coffee Shops

Vancouver is known for its coffee culture, so take some time to explore the many independent coffee shops scattered throughout the city. Grab a book or a friend and spend the afternoon enjoying a warm cup of coffee or tea.

2. Brewery Tours

Vancouver is home to many breweries, and taking a tour is a great way to spend a rainy day. Some of the most popular breweries include Granville Island Brewing, Steamworks Brewing, and Parallel 49 Brewing.

3. Food Tours

Explore Vancouver’s diverse food scene by taking a food tour. Some of the most popular tours include the Granville Island Market Tour, the Gastown Food Tour, and the Vancouver Foodie Tour.

Shopping

When the rain is pouring, it’s always a good time to do some indoor shopping.

1. Pacific Centre

Pacific Centre is Vancouver’s premier shopping destination, with over 100 stores including Nordstrom, Harry Rosen, and Holt Renfrew.

2. Robson Street

Robson Street is Vancouver’s most famous shopping district, with a mix of local and international brands. You’ll find everything from high-end fashion to unique souvenirs.

Museum and Gallery

Vancouver has a rich cultural scene, and a rainy day is the perfect time to explore it.

1. Museum of Anthropology

The Museum of Anthropology at the University of British Columbia is home to over 500,000 objects from around the world, including works by the Indigenous people of Canada’s Pacific Northwest.

2. Vancouver Art Gallery

The Vancouver Art Gallery is one of Canada’s largest art museums, with a collection of over 12,000 works of art. From contemporary to classic, there’s something for everyone.

Conclusion

Don’t let the rain discourage you from enjoying Vancouver. There are plenty of activities to do indoors and outdoors, making it a great destination no matter what the weather. Use this guide to explore the city in a different way, and make the most out of your rainy day.
