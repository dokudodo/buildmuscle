How to Do Push Ups Day: A Complete Guide | Blog

Push ups are one of the most effective exercises to strengthen your upper body, core, and arms. However, many people struggle with push ups, and often give up because they don't know how to do them correctly. In this guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to know about how to do push ups day.

How to Do Push Ups Day: Step-by-Step Guide

Before starting your push ups day, it's important to warm up your muscles. You can do some light cardio, such as jumping jacks or jogging in place, to get your heart rate up and your blood flowing.

Now, let's get started with the push ups:

  1. Get into a plank position. Your hands should be shoulder-width apart, and your feet should be hip-width apart.
  2. Lower your body by bending your elbows. Keep your back straight, and your core engaged.
  3. Push yourself back up to the starting position, using your chest, arms, and shoulders.
  4. Repeat this movement for as many reps as you can.

Make sure to take breaks as needed, and don't push yourself too hard. You can gradually increase the number of reps as you get stronger.

Modified Push Ups

If you are new to push ups, or have difficulty with the standard push up, you can start with modified push ups:

  1. Get into a plank position, but instead of being on your toes, lower your knees to the ground.
  2. Lower your body by bending your elbows, still keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
  3. Push yourself back up to the starting position, using your chest, arms, and shoulders.
  4. Repeat this movement for as many reps as you can.

Tips for Doing Push Ups Day

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your push ups day:

  • Focus on your form. Keep your back straight and your core engaged.
  • Breathe correctly. Inhale as you lower your body, and exhale as you push yourself back up.
  • Start slow. Don't try to do too many reps at once, especially if you are new to push ups.
  • Gradually increase the number of reps as you get stronger.
  • Take breaks as needed. It's better to do a few reps with good form than to do a lot of reps with bad form.

Common Problems and Solutions

Here are some common problems people encounter while doing push ups day, and how to solve them:

  • Problem: Can't do a full push up.
  • Solution: Start with modified push ups, and gradually work your way up to full push ups.
  • Problem: Lower back pain.
  • Solution: Focus on keeping your core engaged, and don't let your lower back sag.
  • Problem: Shoulder pain.
  • Solution: Make sure your hands are in the correct position, and don't let your shoulders hunch up towards your ears.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about push ups day:

Q: How many push ups should I do?
A: It depends on your fitness level. Start with a number that feels comfortable, and gradually increase as you get stronger.
Q: Can push ups help me lose weight?
A: Push ups can help you build muscle, which can boost your metabolism and help you burn more calories. However, they should be combined with a healthy diet and other forms of exercise for optimal weight loss.
Q: Can push ups be bad for your wrists?
A: Push ups can put strain on your wrists, especially if you have weak wrists or are doing them incorrectly. Make sure to keep your hands in the correct position, and consult a doctor or physical therapist if you experience pain.
Q: Can push ups help me build muscle?
A: Yes, push ups are a great exercise for building muscle in your chest, arms, and shoulders. They can also help strengthen your core.

Pros and Cons of Push Ups Day

Here are some pros and cons of doing push ups day:

Pros:

  • Effective for building upper body and core strength.
  • No equipment required.
  • Can be done anywhere.
  • Can be modified for different fitness levels.

Cons:

  • Can be difficult for beginners.
  • Can put strain on wrists and shoulders.
  • May not be effective for building lower body strength.
  • May not be suitable for people with certain injuries or health conditions.

Overall, push ups are a great exercise to incorporate into your fitness routine, as long as you do them correctly and listen to your body. With practice and patience, you can build strength and improve your overall fitness.
