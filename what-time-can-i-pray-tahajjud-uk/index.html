Are you wondering what time can you pray Tahajjud in the UK? Tahajjud is a voluntary prayer that is performed after the Isha prayer and before the Fajr prayer. It is a special time when the gates of heaven are open, and Allah is most merciful. This article will guide you on what time to pray Tahajjud in the UK, how to perform it, and its benefits.

What time can I pray Tahajjud in the UK?

The time for Tahajjud prayer starts after the Isha prayer and lasts till the Fajr prayer. In the UK, the time for Isha prayer varies depending on the season. During winter, Isha prayer starts around 6 pm and during summer, it starts around 10 pm. The time for Fajr prayer also varies depending on the season. During winter, it starts around 6 am, and during summer, it starts around 3 am.

Therefore, the best time to pray Tahajjud in the UK is after the Isha prayer and before the Fajr prayer. You can check the exact time for Isha and Fajr prayers in your local mosque or by using prayer time apps.

How to perform Tahajjud prayer?

The method of performing Tahajjud prayer is similar to other voluntary prayers. Here are the steps:

  1. Perform Wudu (ablution)
  2. Make the intention for Tahajjud prayer
  3. Perform two or more Rakat (units) of prayer
  4. Recite Surah Al-Fatiha and a portion of the Quran in each Rakat
  5. Make Dua (supplication) after the prayer

You can perform Tahajjud prayer in any part of the night, but it is recommended to perform it in the last third of the night. This is because Allah descends to the lowest heaven during this time and accepts the prayers of His servants.

What are the benefits of Tahajjud prayer?

Tahajjud prayer has numerous benefits, both spiritual and physical. Here are some of them:

  • It strengthens your relationship with Allah
  • It increases your faith and spirituality
  • It helps you overcome sins and temptations
  • It brings peace and tranquility to your heart
  • It improves your mental and physical health

Tips for praying Tahajjud in the UK

Here are some tips for praying Tahajjud in the UK:

  • Make a habit of waking up during the last third of the night
  • Take a short nap during the day to help you stay awake at night
  • Recite the Quran and make Dua during Tahajjud prayer
  • Find a quiet and comfortable place to pray
  • Try to make Tahajjud prayer a regular habit

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I pray Tahajjud after Fajr prayer?

No, you cannot pray Tahajjud after Fajr prayer. The time for Tahajjud prayer ends with the start of Fajr prayer.

Do I have to perform Tahajjud prayer every night?

No, Tahajjud prayer is a voluntary prayer, and it is not obligatory. You can perform it whenever you want, but it is recommended to perform it regularly.

Can I perform Tahajjud prayer alone or with others?

You can perform Tahajjud prayer alone or with others. It is recommended to perform it alone as it allows you to focus more on your prayer and have a deeper connection with Allah.

What is the significance of the last third of the night?

The last third of the night is a special time when Allah descends to the lowest heaven and accepts the prayers of His servants. It is a time when the gates of heaven are open, and Allah is most merciful.

Can women perform Tahajjud prayer?

Yes, women can perform Tahajjud prayer. There is no restriction on women performing voluntary prayers.
