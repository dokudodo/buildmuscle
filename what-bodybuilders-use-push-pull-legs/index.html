What is Push Pull Legs?

Push pull legs is a workout routine that involves dividing your exercises into three categories: push movements, pull movements, and leg movements. The idea is to train your upper body push muscles (chest, shoulders, and triceps) on one day, your upper body pull muscles (back and biceps) on the next day, and your leg muscles (quads, hamstrings, and calves) on the third day.

This type of training allows for more focus on each muscle group and also gives ample time for recovery between each workout. Additionally, it allows for greater flexibility in scheduling your workouts and ensures that you are not overtraining any particular muscle group.

How to Use Push Pull Legs

Before starting any new workout routine, it is important to assess your current fitness level and set realistic goals. Once you have done so, you can begin to implement push pull legs into your routine.

Step by Step Guide

  1. Choose the days you will work out and which muscle groups you will focus on each day.
  2. Select exercises that target each muscle group and plan your workouts accordingly.
  3. Begin each workout with a warm-up and end with a cool-down to prevent injury.
  4. Progressively increase the weight or reps of each exercise over time to continue challenging your muscles.
  5. Rest for at least one day between each workout to allow for proper recovery.

Tips for Success

While push pull legs can be an effective training method for bodybuilders, there are a few tips to keep in mind for optimal success:

  • Focus on proper form and technique to prevent injury and maximize results.
  • Incorporate a variety of exercises to target each muscle group from different angles.
  • Listen to your body and adjust your workouts as needed if you are experiencing pain or discomfort.
  • Be consistent with your workouts and make sure to rest and recover adequately.

FAQ

What are the benefits of push pull legs?

Push pull legs allows for greater focus on each muscle group, flexibility in scheduling workouts, and ample recovery time between workouts.

Can push pull legs be used for weight loss?

While push pull legs is primarily used for muscle building, it can also aid in weight loss when combined with a healthy diet and cardiovascular exercise.

How often should I do push pull legs?

It is recommended to do push pull legs two to three times per week, with at least one day of rest in between each workout.

Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • Allows for greater focus on each muscle group
  • Flexible scheduling
  • Ample recovery time
  • Can aid in weight loss

Cons:

  • Requires careful planning and organization
  • May not be suitable for beginners
  • Can be time-consuming

In conclusion, push pull legs is a popular training method for bodybuilders that allows for greater focus on each muscle group, flexibility in scheduling, and ample recovery time. By following a few simple tips, you can successfully implement push pull legs into your workout routine and achieve your fitness goals.
