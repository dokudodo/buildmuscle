What Are The Simple 6 Exercises For Relaxed English Language

Are you looking for an easy way to improve your English speaking skills? Look no further than the Simple 6 exercises! In this post, we’ll cover what the Simple 6 exercises are, how to do them, and the benefits of incorporating them into your language learning routine.

What are the Simple 6 exercises?

The Simple 6 exercises are a series of six exercises designed to help individuals improve their English speaking skills. These exercises focus on various aspects of language learning, such as pronunciation, intonation, and grammar.

The six exercises are as follows:

  1. Repeat after me
  2. Slow down and focus on pronunciation
  3. Listen and repeat
  4. Ask and answer questions
  5. Describe a picture
  6. Tell a story

How to do the Simple 6 exercises

Now that you know what the Simple 6 exercises are, let’s dive into how to do them.

1. Repeat after me

This exercise involves listening to a native speaker say a word or phrase and then repeating it back. It’s a simple but effective way to improve pronunciation and intonation.

To do this exercise, find a native speaker or a recording of a native speaker saying words or phrases. Listen carefully and then repeat what you hear. You can start with simple words and phrases and work your way up to more complex ones.

2. Slow down and focus on pronunciation

When speaking English, it’s important to slow down and focus on your pronunciation. This exercise involves speaking slowly and deliberately, focusing on accurately pronouncing each word.

To do this exercise, choose a short passage or sentence and read it aloud. As you read, focus on each word and how it’s pronounced. Make sure to speak slowly and deliberately, emphasizing each syllable.

3. Listen and repeat

This exercise is similar to the first one, but instead of repeating after a native speaker, you’ll be repeating after yourself. It’s a great way to practice speaking without the pressure of having to come up with new words and phrases on the spot.

To do this exercise, record yourself speaking a sentence or two. Listen to the recording and then repeat what you said. Focus on your pronunciation and intonation, and try to improve with each repetition.

4. Ask and answer questions

Asking and answering questions is an important part of conversation. This exercise involves practicing this skill by asking and answering a series of questions.

To do this exercise, find a partner or use a language learning app that provides conversation prompts. Take turns asking and answering questions, focusing on using correct grammar and pronunciation.

5. Describe a picture

Describing a picture is a great way to practice using adjectives and descriptive language. This exercise involves looking at a picture and describing what you see.

To do this exercise, find a picture online or in a book. Look at the picture and then describe what you see using as many adjectives as possible. Focus on using correct grammar and pronunciation.

6. Tell a story

Telling a story is a fun and engaging way to practice speaking English. This exercise involves telling a story from start to finish.

To do this exercise, choose a topic or prompt and then tell a story about it. Focus on using correct grammar and pronunciation, and try to make the story as engaging as possible.

Tips for the Simple 6 exercises

Here are a few tips to help you get the most out of the Simple 6 exercises:

  • Start with the easier exercises and work your way up to the more challenging ones
  • Record yourself speaking and listen back to identify areas for improvement
  • Practice regularly to see the most improvement
  • Find a language partner or tutor to practice with
  • Use language learning apps or websites to supplement your practice

Solution for the Simple 6 exercises

The solution to improving your English speaking skills lies in consistent practice. Incorporating the Simple 6 exercises into your language learning routine is a great way to practice regularly and see improvement over time.

Remember to start with the easier exercises and work your way up to the more challenging ones. Use the tips provided to help you get the most out of each exercise, and don’t be afraid to make mistakes – they’re a natural part of the learning process.

FAQ about the Simple 6 exercises

What if I don’t have a language partner?

You can use language learning apps or websites to find a language partner or tutor. Alternatively, you can practice on your own by recording yourself and listening back to identify areas for improvement.

How often should I practice the Simple 6 exercises?

It’s recommended to practice the Simple 6 exercises at least three times a week for best results.

Can I do the Simple 6 exercises on my own?

Yes, you can do the Simple 6 exercises on your own. However, practicing with a language partner or tutor can be helpful for receiving feedback and practicing conversation skills.

Pros and Cons of the Simple 6 exercises

Pros:

  • Easy to do
  • Effective for improving English speaking skills
  • Can be done on your own or with a partner
  • Focuses on various aspects of language learning

Cons:

  • May not be challenging enough for advanced English speakers
  • Requires consistent practice to see improvement
  • May require additional resources for more advanced exercises

Overall, the Simple 6 exercises are a great way to improve your English speaking skills. Incorporate them into your language learning routine and see improvement over time!
