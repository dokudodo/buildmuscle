Are you interested in taking up ballet as a hobby or profession? Knowing the basic ballet moves list is essential for building a strong foundation. In this article, we will guide you through the most popular ballet moves list, how to execute them correctly, and some tips to help you perfect your technique.

Plie

Plie is a basic ballet move that helps to warm up the legs and develop strength in the thighs. To perform a plie, stand with your feet hip-width apart, toes turned outwards. Bend your knees, keeping your back straight and your heels on the ground. Keep your arms in front of you, slightly rounded, and your shoulders relaxed.

Tip: Make sure to keep your knees aligned with your toes and avoid letting them collapse inwards.

Tendu

Tendu is a fundamental ballet move that helps to develop foot and leg flexibility. To perform a tendu, stand with your feet in first position (heels together, toes turned outwards). Extend one foot forward or to the side, keeping your toes pointed and your heel on the ground. Return your foot to first position. Repeat with the other foot.

Tip: Make sure to keep your hips and shoulders aligned with your feet and avoid letting them twist or turn.

Fondu

Fondu is a graceful ballet move that helps to strengthen the legs and improve balance. To perform a fondu, stand with your feet in first position. Bend one knee, lifting your foot off the ground, while extending the other leg. Return to first position. Repeat with the other leg.

Tip: Keep your back straight and your core engaged. Avoid leaning forward or backward.

Frappe

Frappe is a fast and energetic ballet move that helps to develop foot speed and agility. To perform a frappe, stand with your feet in first position. Point one foot forward and quickly strike the ground with your toes, then return to first position. Repeat with the other foot.

Tip: Keep your knees straight and your movements controlled. Avoid letting your body sway or lose balance.

Grand Battement

Grand Battement is a dramatic ballet move that involves lifting one leg high into the air. To perform a grand battement, stand with your feet in first position. Lift one leg straight up, keeping your toes pointed and your upper body straight. Lower your leg to the ground. Repeat with the other leg.

Tip: Keep your standing leg straight and your core engaged. Avoid leaning or tilting your upper body.

Pirouette

Pirouette is a classic ballet move that involves spinning on one foot. To perform a pirouette, stand with your feet in first position. Step forward on one foot, then lift your other foot off the ground and turn your body. Bring your lifted foot back to first position.

Tip: Keep your eyes focused on a fixed point and your arms rounded. Practice your spotting technique to avoid dizziness.

Chaine

Chaine is a fun and playful ballet move that involves spinning in a circle. To perform a chaine, stand with your feet in first position. Step forward on one foot, then spin on the ball of that foot while bringing your other foot forward. Continue the spin by stepping forward and repeating the movement.

Tip: Keep your arms rounded and your movements light and controlled. Practice your balance and coordination.

Jete

Jete is a dynamic ballet move that involves leaping into the air. To perform a jete, stand with your feet in first position. Jump off one foot and extend your other leg forward, then land on the same foot. Repeat with the other foot.

Tip: Keep your arms rounded and your movements controlled. Practice your landing technique to avoid injury.

Arabesque

Arabesque is a beautiful ballet move that involves extending one leg behind you while standing on the other leg. To perform an arabesque, stand with your feet in first position. Lift one leg behind you, keeping it straight and your toes pointed. Hold your arms in front of you, slightly rounded.

Tip: Keep your back straight and your core engaged. Avoid leaning or tilting your upper body.

Grand Jete

Grand Jete is a impressive ballet move that involves leaping into the air and splitting your legs. To perform a grand jete, stand with your feet in first position. Jump off one foot and extend your other leg forward, then leap into the air and split your legs, landing on the opposite foot.

Tip: Keep your arms rounded and your movements controlled. Practice your landing technique to avoid injury.

Conclusion

Learning the basic ballet moves list is essential for anyone interested in pursuing ballet as a hobby or profession. Practice these moves regularly, focusing on correct technique and control. With time and dedication, you'll be able to master even the most challenging ballet moves.
