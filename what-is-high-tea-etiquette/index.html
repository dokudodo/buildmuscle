High Tea Etiquette A Guide To Enjoying Your Afternoon Tea

High tea is a tradition that has been around for centuries, originating in England in the 18th century. Today, it is still a popular way to enjoy an afternoon with friends or family. However, if you're not familiar with high tea etiquette, it can be a bit overwhelming. In this guide, we'll go over everything you need to know to enjoy your high tea experience to the fullest.

What is High Tea?

High tea is a meal that is typically served in the late afternoon or early evening. It consists of a selection of finger sandwiches, scones with clotted cream and jam, and pastries, all served with a pot of tea. High tea is often seen as a more formal affair than a regular afternoon tea, with a dress code and other etiquette rules.

High Tea Etiquette

Dress Code

When attending a high tea, it's important to dress appropriately. Women should wear a dress or skirt, while men should wear a suit or blazer. Avoid wearing anything too casual, like jeans or sneakers.

Arrival Time

It's important to arrive on time for your high tea reservation. If you're running late, be sure to call ahead and let the restaurant or hotel know.

Seating Arrangements

When you arrive at the high tea venue, you'll be shown to your table. If you're with a group, it's polite to wait until everyone has arrived before sitting down.

Tea Selection

The tea selection is an important part of high tea. It's important to choose a tea that complements the food you'll be eating. If you're not sure what to choose, ask the server for recommendations.

Food Selection

When the food arrives, it's important to use the correct utensils. Use the small fork for sandwiches and the larger fork for pastries. Scones are typically eaten with your hands, so don't be afraid to dig in!

Manners

Good manners are important during high tea. Remember to use your napkin, keep your elbows off the table, and avoid talking with your mouth full.

Tips for Enjoying High Tea

Here are a few additional tips to help you enjoy your high tea experience:

  • Try a variety of teas and foods to get the full experience.
  • Take your time and enjoy the conversation and atmosphere.
  • If you're unsure about something, don't be afraid to ask your server for help.

FAQ

What is the difference between high tea and afternoon tea?

High tea is a more substantial meal that is typically served in the late afternoon or early evening, while afternoon tea is a lighter meal that is served earlier in the afternoon.

What should I wear to high tea?

Women should wear a dress or skirt, while men should wear a suit or blazer.

What kind of tea is served at high tea?

There are many different types of tea that can be served at high tea, including black tea, green tea, and herbal tea.

Pros and Cons of High Tea

Pros

  • High tea is a great way to spend time with friends or family.
  • The food and tea selection is usually excellent.
  • It's a fun way to dress up and feel fancy for an afternoon.

Cons

  • High tea can be expensive.
  • The dress code and etiquette rules can be intimidating.
  • It's not a meal that everyone will enjoy.

Overall, high tea can be a wonderful experience if you're prepared for the etiquette rules and dress code. By following these guidelines, you'll be able to enjoy your high tea experience to the fullest.
