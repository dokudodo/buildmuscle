If you've just given birth, you may be wondering when you can start doing pelvic floor exercises. These exercises can help strengthen the muscles that support your bladder, uterus, and rectum, which can be weakened during pregnancy and childbirth. In this article, we'll discuss how long after birth you can start doing pelvic floor exercises, and provide some tips for getting started.

How to Do Pelvic Floor Exercises

Before we discuss when you can start doing pelvic floor exercises, let's first go over how to do them. The first step is to identify your pelvic floor muscles. These are the muscles that you use to stop the flow of urine midstream. To locate them, try stopping and starting your urine flow while going to the bathroom.

Once you've identified your pelvic floor muscles, you can start doing the exercises. Start by tightening these muscles for a few seconds, and then relax them for a few seconds. Repeat this several times in a row, and try to work up to holding the contraction for 10 seconds at a time.

How Long After Birth Can You Do Pelvic Floor Exercises?

The answer to this question depends on a few factors, including the type of birth you had and any complications you experienced. In general, it's safe to start doing pelvic floor exercises as soon as you feel comfortable. However, if you had a vaginal delivery, you may want to wait a few days to a week before starting these exercises. If you had a cesarean delivery, you can start doing them as soon as you feel comfortable.

It's important to listen to your body when starting pelvic floor exercises. If you experience any pain or discomfort, stop and talk to your doctor or midwife. They can help you determine if you need to wait a bit longer before starting these exercises.

Tips for Doing Pelvic Floor Exercises

Here are a few tips to help you get started with pelvic floor exercises:

  • Start with just a few repetitions at a time, and work up to doing more as you get stronger.
  • Try to do these exercises throughout the day, rather than all at once.
  • Don't hold your breath while doing these exercises. Breathe normally.
  • If you're having trouble identifying your pelvic floor muscles, ask your doctor or midwife for help.

FAQs

Can pelvic floor exercises help with incontinence?

Yes, doing pelvic floor exercises can help strengthen the muscles that control your bladder, which can reduce your risk of developing incontinence.

Can men do pelvic floor exercises?

Yes, men can also benefit from doing pelvic floor exercises. These exercises can help with urinary incontinence, erectile dysfunction, and other issues.

Pros and Cons

Here are a few pros and cons to consider when deciding whether to start doing pelvic floor exercises:

Pros:

  • Pelvic floor exercises can help reduce your risk of incontinence and other issues.
  • These exercises can be done anywhere, at any time.
  • They're easy to do and don't require any special equipment.

Cons:

  • It can be difficult to remember to do these exercises regularly.
  • If you have a pelvic floor disorder, these exercises may not be recommended.
  • If you're not doing the exercises correctly, they may not be effective.

Overall, pelvic floor exercises can be a great way to improve your pelvic health after giving birth. If you're not sure whether these exercises are right for you, talk to your doctor or midwife. They can help you determine the best course of action based on your individual needs and circumstances.
