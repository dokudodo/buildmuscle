What Are The Basic Exercises Performed In The Barre

Barre workouts have become increasingly popular in recent years as a low-impact way to tone muscles and improve flexibility. The barre is a stationary handrail used for balance and support during the workout. In this article, we will cover the basic exercises performed in the barre.

Plié

The plié is a basic warm-up exercise in barre workouts. It involves bending the knees while keeping the back straight and the heels on the ground. This exercise targets the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. You can perform a plié in first position (heels together, toes turned out), second position (feet shoulder-width apart, toes turned out), or third position (one foot in front of the other, toes turned out).

How to do a Plié:

  1. Stand with your feet in first, second, or third position.
  2. Place your hands on the barre for balance.
  3. Bend your knees while keeping your back straight.
  4. Lower your body until your thighs are parallel to the ground.
  5. Slowly rise back up to starting position.

Tips: Keep your heels on the ground and your knees tracking over your toes. Engage your core muscles to maintain balance.

Pros:

  • Strengthens lower body muscles
  • Improves balance and stability
  • Can be modified for different fitness levels

Cons:

  • May be difficult for those with knee or hip problems
  • Requires a barre or sturdy surface for support

Relevé

The relevé is a standing exercise that involves lifting onto the balls of the feet. This exercise targets the calves, ankles, and core muscles. You can perform a relevé in first position, second position, or fifth position (heels together, toes pointed out).

How to do a Relevé:

  1. Stand with your feet in first, second, or fifth position.
  2. Place your hands on the barre for balance.
  3. Lift onto the balls of your feet.
  4. Hold for a few seconds, then lower back down.

Tips: Keep your heels together and your knees straight. Engage your core muscles to maintain balance.

Pros:

  • Strengthens lower legs and ankles
  • Improves balance and stability
  • Can be done anywhere without equipment

Cons:

  • May be difficult for those with foot or ankle problems
  • May not provide enough resistance for advanced fitness levels

Tendu

The tendu is a standing exercise that involves sliding one foot along the ground while keeping the other foot in place. This exercise targets the inner and outer thighs, as well as the glutes and core muscles. You can perform a tendu in first, second, or fifth position.

How to do a Tendu:

  1. Stand with your feet in first, second, or fifth position.
  2. Place your hands on the barre for balance.
  3. Slide one foot along the ground while keeping the other foot in place.
  4. Return to starting position.

Tips: Keep your hips level and your back straight. Engage your core muscles to maintain balance.

Pros:

  • Targets inner and outer thigh muscles
  • Improves balance and coordination
  • Can be modified for different fitness levels

Cons:

  • May be difficult for those with hip or knee problems
  • Requires a barre or sturdy surface for support

Dégage

The dégage is a standing exercise that involves lifting one leg off the ground while keeping the other leg in place. This exercise targets the glutes, hamstrings, and core muscles. You can perform a dégage in first, second, or fifth position.

How to do a Dégage:

  1. Stand with your feet in first, second, or fifth position.
  2. Place your hands on the barre for balance.
  3. Lift one leg off the ground while keeping the other leg in place.
  4. Lower the lifted leg back down.

Tips: Keep your hips level and your back straight. Engage your core muscles to maintain balance.

Pros:

  • Targets glutes and hamstrings
  • Improves balance and coordination
  • Can be modified for different fitness levels

Cons:

  • May be difficult for those with hip or knee problems
  • Requires a barre or sturdy surface for support

Rond de Jambe

The rond de jambe is a standing exercise that involves circling one leg while keeping the other leg in place. This exercise targets the inner and outer thighs, as well as the glutes and core muscles. You can perform a rond de jambe in first, second, or fifth position.

How to do a Rond de Jambe:

  1. Stand with your feet in first, second, or fifth position.
  2. Place your hands on the barre for balance.
  3. Circle one leg in front of the other, then behind.
  4. Switch legs and repeat.

Tips: Keep your hips level and your back straight. Engage your core muscles to maintain balance.

Pros:

  • Targets inner and outer thigh muscles
  • Improves balance and coordination
  • Can be modified for different fitness levels

Cons:

  • May be difficult for those with hip or knee problems
  • Requires a barre or sturdy surface for support

Grand Battement

The grand battement is a standing exercise that involves lifting one leg to hip height or higher. This exercise targets the glutes, hamstrings, and core muscles. You can perform a grand battement in first, second, or fifth position.

How to do a Grand Battement:

  1. Stand with your feet in first, second, or fifth position.
  2. Place your hands on the barre for balance.
  3. Lift one leg to hip height or higher.
  4. Lower the lifted leg back down.

Tips: Keep your hips level and your back straight. Engage your core muscles to maintain balance.

Pros:

  • Targets glutes and hamstrings
  • Improves balance and coordination
  • Can be modified for different fitness levels

Cons:

  • May be difficult for those with hip or knee problems
  • Requires a barre or sturdy surface for support
