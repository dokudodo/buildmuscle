What is the Upper Chest?

Your chest is made up of two main muscles: the pectoralis major and the pectoralis minor. The pectoralis major is the larger of the two and is responsible for the bulk of your chest muscle mass. The upper chest, also known as the clavicular head, is the upper portion of the pectoralis major that connects to your collarbone.

Targeting your upper chest can help create a well-rounded, defined chest and improve your overall upper body strength. It is important to note that while the upper chest is often the most neglected area of the chest, it is just as important as the rest of the chest muscles.

Benefits of Upper Chest Workouts

Some benefits of including upper chest workouts in your exercise routine include:

  • Improved upper body strength
  • More defined, well-rounded chest
  • Improved posture and alignment
  • Reduced risk of injury in the shoulder and chest area

Best Upper Chest Exercises

Here are some of the most effective bodyweight exercises for targeting your upper chest:

Push-Ups

Push-ups are a classic bodyweight exercise that effectively target your entire chest, including the upper chest. To target your upper chest specifically, try doing a variation of push-ups where your hands are placed wider than shoulder-width apart and your elbows are tucked in close to your body.

Diamond Push-Ups

Diamond push-ups are a variation of push-ups that place more emphasis on your triceps and upper chest. To do a diamond push-up, place your hands together in a diamond shape in front of your chest and lower yourself down until your chest touches your hands.

Pike Push-Ups

Pike push-ups are a great exercise for targeting your shoulders and upper chest. To do a pike push-up, start in a downward dog position with your hands and feet on the ground, then lower your head down towards the ground and push back up.

Decline Push-Ups

Decline push-ups are another great variation of push-ups that place more emphasis on your upper chest. To do a decline push-up, place your feet on an elevated surface such as a bench or box and your hands on the ground, then lower yourself down and push back up.

Tips for Proper Form

When doing upper chest exercises, it is important to maintain proper form to avoid injury and get the most out of your workout. Here are some tips for proper form:

  • Keep your core engaged and back straight
  • Breathe in on the way down and exhale on the way up
  • Keep your elbows tucked in close to your body
  • Don't let your shoulders hunch up towards your ears

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Some common mistakes to avoid when doing upper chest exercises include:

  • Arching your back or letting your hips sag
  • Letting your elbows flare out to the sides
  • Not engaging your core or using momentum to complete the movement

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about upper chest workouts:

Can I only do bodyweight exercises to target my upper chest?

Yes, bodyweight exercises can be just as effective as using weights to target your upper chest. However, using weights can provide an extra challenge and help you progress faster.

How often should I do upper chest workouts?

You should aim to do upper chest workouts 1-2 times per week, along with other chest exercises and a variety of upper body workouts.

Will upper chest workouts make my chest look too bulky?

No, upper chest workouts will not necessarily make your chest look too bulky. It is important to have a well-rounded workout routine that targets all areas of your body to avoid any imbalances. Remember, it is also important to maintain a healthy diet and overall lifestyle to achieve your desired physique.

Pros and Cons of Bodyweight Upper Chest Workouts

Pros:

  • No equipment necessary
  • Can be done anywhere
  • Effective for targeting upper chest muscles

Cons:

  • May not provide enough of a challenge for advanced lifters
  • May require more reps to see results compared to using weights
  • May not provide as much variety compared to using weights

Overall, bodyweight exercises are a great option for targeting your upper chest muscles. By incorporating these exercises into your workout routine and following proper form and technique, you can achieve a well-defined, strong upper chest.
